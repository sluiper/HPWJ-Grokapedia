# AS/NZS 4233 + Australian Competency Units – Deep Dive

**Standard**: AS/NZS 4233.1:2013 High pressure water jetting systems – Safe operation and maintenance  
**Part 2**: Construction and performance requirements

## National Units of Competency (Training.gov.au)
- **MSMWJ202 / MSMSS00017 – Use high pressure water jetting equipment**  
  Skill set for Class B operations (assistant / basic operator). Includes permits, emergency procedures, equipment use.
- **MSMWJ301 / MSMSS00018 – Operate a high pressure water jetting system**  
  Full operator competency. Allows unsupervised work and supervision of assistants. Prerequisite: previous unit or equivalent.

## Key Requirements from AS/NZS 4233.1 and Safe Work Australia Guidance
- Only competent personnel shall operate or supervise HPWJ systems.
- Training by a competent person / Registered Training Organisation (RTO).
- Trainers for Class B: accredited by RTO + minimum 5 years relevant experience.
- **Refresher / Verification of Competency (VOC)**: At least every 2 years (mandatory in many jurisdictions, e.g. Victoria WorkSafe).
- Medical Alert Cards: Strongly promoted; many RTOs issue them as standard.
- Training topics (Safe Work Australia table): system operation, hazards, emergency actions, equipment care, PPE, risk management, etc.

## Class Distinction
- Class A: Lower pressure / less complex
- Class B: High pressure industrial (most Anabeeb work falls here)

## Strengths for Anabeeb
- Formal national units of competency that can be referenced in TVTC documentation.
- Clear 2-year VOC cycle (stricter and more frequent than some other frameworks – preferred for high-risk petrochemical).
- Strong WHS integration and explicit guidance on what “competent” looks like.
- Medical alert emphasis aligns perfectly with Chapter 10.

## Recommendation
Adopt the 2-year VOC cycle as Anabeeb standard. Map MSMWJ301 learning outcomes to the Anabeeb Level 2 Operator assessment checklist. Use Safe Work Australia training topics table as a completeness check for ATC curriculum.
