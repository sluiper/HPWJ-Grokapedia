## 1. Objective & Scope

Ensure all HPWJ activities are conducted with safe operating practices, safeguard users, and achieve consistent results. Covers automatic, semi-automatic and manual methods. Prior evaluation of alternatives to HPWJ is required. Flow: Evaluate → Plan → Prepare → Risk Assessment → Site Prep → Set-up → Pre-Job Checklist → Execute → Restore.

**Pressure Definitions (OPS-P-019 §3.8)**
- LP: < 3 000 psi (200 bar)
- HP: 3 000 – 20 000 psi (1 380 bar)
- UHP: up to 36 250 psi (2 500 bar) in definition text; **maximum allowable for any HPWJ activity = 40 000 psi (2 759 bar)**. No work above this.
- General water jetting term covers up to 25 000 psi; above that is UHP.

**MCR-046 (new):** Maximum system pressure for Anabeeb HPWJ activities shall not exceed 40 000 psi (2 759 bar).

---

## 2. Definitions (Key Operational)

- **Dump Valve:** Manually operated fail-safe valve requiring continuous pressure by lance operator; stops/diverts/lowers pressure on release.
- **Lancing:** Rigid or flexible lance + nozzle inserted/retracted inside pipe/tube.
- **Moleing:** Hose/lance/nozzle for internal drain/pipe cleaning (also called rodding).
- **Shotgunning:** Hand-held gun/lance directed in all planes. Barrel **minimum 1.2 m** from rearmost trigger to nozzle tip.
- **Competence:** Sufficient training + experience/knowledge for the complexity of the task; able to make informed decisions and follow Safe System of Work.
- **Safety Chain / Whip-check:** Fitted to all end connections of HP hoses to prevent whipping on separation.

## 5. Duties & Responsibilities (OPS-P-019 §6)

| Role | Key Accountabilities |
|------|----------------------|
| **Project Manager / Project in Charge** | Overall responsibility that decision tree is followed; operators trained, certified and fit. **Cannot be delegated.** |
| **Site Supervisor – HPWJ** | Visual pre-op checklist; hoses free of broken/rusted braid; O-rings/gaskets good; filters; rupture disc ≤1.2× MAWP of lowest component; dump/trigger correct; nozzles not worn/plugged; fittings/lances/stingers rated ≥ pump MAWP. |
| **HSE Supervisor** | Immediate stop-work authority for any dangerous activity; practical advice. |
| **HPWJ Team Members** | **Minimum 3 members**. Direct line of sight between machine operator and jetting operator. One dedicated to EMERGENCY STOP when line of sight lost. All aware and able to react. Controlled by suitably trained competent person. |
| **HP Jetter Operator** | Valid current certification for the process; hand signals; coordination. |
| **Emergency Button Operator** | Direct line of sight of both HPWJ operator and pump operator; sole task is emergency reaction. |

**MCR-047 (new):** Anabeeb HPWJ teams shall consist of a minimum of three competent persons. One shall be dedicated to the emergency stop / dump when line of sight between lance and pump is lost.

**MCR-048 (new):** Rupture disc shall be rated no greater than 1.2 times the MAWP of the lowest-rated component in the system.

## 6. Training (OPS-P-019 §7)

- Valid current certification relevant to the type of HPWJ activity, issued by Anabeeb Training Department, verified by HPWJ Supervisor before start.
- Manual water jetting requires demonstrated knowledge, skill and experience.
- **Minimum 2 years continuous operation experience** required for HP Jetters authorised for Manual Operation; identified on training card.
- In-house topics: cutting action of jet, nozzle/pressure selection, PPE, unit inspection, 10k/20k basic ops, TLE/LTC & IBC/OBC/Bundle Blaster, primary equipment purpose, system operation + lance handling + emergency shutdown + signals, safety devices, precautions, water supply/electrical hazards/grounding/wastewater, plus any project-specific.
- All HP hoses, guns, flexible lances, foot valves, pressure relief valves pressure-tested; records maintained and submitted during plant inspection.

**MCR-049 (new):** Manual HPWJ operators shall have minimum two years continuous relevant experience, recorded on training card, in addition to method-specific certification.

## 7. Semi-Automated & Automated Lancing (OPS-P-019 §8)

Preferred methods (aligns with SABIC hierarchy):
- Lance Tube Cleaner 2XL / 3XL
- Inside Bundle Cleaner (IBC)
- Outside Bundle Cleaner (OBC)
- Rigid Lancing Machines
- Tank cleaning heads / 3D nozzles / Gamma jets / Rotary lances
- Bundle Blaster

Safety controls: Flow Control (Diverter) Valve + Foot Pedal (Dump Valve). Set up per manufacturer. Operator controls from remote position. Removes personnel from jet exposure → higher safety + productivity.

## 8. Manual Operation (OPS-P-019 §9) – Strict Controls

**Management approval required before any manual lancing / gun operation.**

- Min 3-man unit, personnel changes minimised.
- Daily Safety Checklist completed before start and each shift.
- Examine equipment to be cleaned for debris, corrosive/toxic chemicals, flammable liquids/vapours.
- Hard barriers by Operations; shields over back end of exchanger tubes; adjacent equipment guarded.
- System flushed prior to fitting nozzle.
- Correct PPE for pressure class before starting.
- Minimise poor footing (mud, slippery surfaces); good housekeeping.
- Increase pressure slowly and controlled.
- Clear signals agreed for start / increase / decrease / shutdown. Supervisor ensures understanding.
- Person closest to nozzle has direct control of dump / dead-man / foot valve.
- Lance length **minimum 1.2 m** from butt to nozzle (shorter only under special RA). Double-trigger gun considered for UHP short barrels.
- **Trigger mechanism must never be locked, wedged or blocked ON.**
- No person besides operators within **10 metres** of operating equipment without permission. System de-pressured if unauthorised person enters.
- Hand-held shotgunning **not above 10 000 psi (680 bar)** with nozzle orifice larger than 1.6 mm (reaction force control). Body weight + reaction force considered for any hand-held.
- Reaction force formula identical: Back Thrust (lbf) = 0.052 × GPM × √psi; ≤ 250 N.
- Never direct lance toward self or another person.
- Burst Protection Sleeve required where UHP hose joins hand-held device.
- Multiple manual ops separated by ≥6 m or adequate physical barrier.
- Lance/hose marked with paint/tape **no closer than 600 mm** from nozzle.
- Rigid section / stinger at end of flexible hose of sufficient length to prevent nozzle turning around inside pipe; total lance+nozzle length > pipe ID.
- **Anti-withdrawal devices (back-out preventer & hose/lance catcher) mandatory** for all lancing/pipe cleaning; attached to pipe/tube bundle. Vertical hose catcher for vertical exchangers.
- Nozzle inserted before pressurising; removed after depressurising.
- No bolts/unsecured material used as hole markers (projectile risk).
- Equipment being cleaned secured (not hand-held).
- Hoses laid straight; not over roadways without ramps; whip-checks at joints; tied off if drop >3 m.
- Supervisors aware of pressure losses in additional hose lengths (over-pressure risk).
- Any leak → cease, depressurise, then repair.
- Earthing/grounding to prevent static; anti-seize on stainless fittings.
- **No water jetting ever from a ladder.**

**MCR-050 (new):** Hand-held shotgunning shall not be performed above 10 000 psi with orifice >1.6 mm. Reaction force ≤250 N absolute.

**MCR-051 (new):** No unauthorised person within 10 m of operating Anabeeb HPWJ equipment; system depressurised on breach.

**MCR-052 (new):** Lance/hose shall be marked no closer than 600 mm from the nozzle tip.

## 9. Jetting in Confined Space (OPS-P-019)

- Prefer alternative method or fully remove jetter (robot / remote tank head).
- If required: full CSE permit + emergency arrangements + gas testing + continuous monitoring + ventilation + lighting + manhole watch + communication + rescue plan + personal gas meter + fall arrest + harness/lifeline + continuous draining of effluent.
- Electrical ≤25 V.
- Surface-supplied breathing air + chemical/slicker suits where applicable; only trained operators.
- Prefer hydraulic/pneumatic 2D/3D heads with proper control valve and securing.
- Platform/tray integrity assessment before work.
- Separate RA for high-pressure/UHP gun inside confined space (barrel length, double trigger, proximity to others).
- Grounding for static.

Maps to existing Chapter 6 + MCR-026 + confined-space sections of Ch5/Ch8.
