# Global HPWJ Standards & Competency Overview (Deep Dive Summary)

**Last Updated**: 16 July 2026

This folder consolidates maximum available public information on the world’s leading high-pressure water jetting standards, codes of practice, and competency frameworks. It supports Chapter 13 and all training design at Anabeeb ATC.

## Key Global Frameworks

1. **WJTA (USA)** – Industry Best Practices ("Orange Book") + Hydroblaster Certification Program
2. **WJA (UK)** – Codes of Practice (Blue / Black) + City & Guilds accredited training pathway
3. **Australia** – AS/NZS 4233.1:2013 + National Units of Competency (MSMWJ series)
4. **Netherlands – SIR** – Industrial cleaning / water jetting certification benchmark
5. **Europe** – EWJI harmonisation efforts + national bodies (DIRV Germany etc.)
6. **Manufacturer** – NLB, StoneAge, Hammelmann, Peinemann, DERC Salotech equipment-specific

See individual Summary files in this folder for detailed extracts.

## Common Themes Across All Leading Standards
- Mandatory formal training before unsupervised operation
- Combination of theory (hazards, physics, injuries, PPE, risk assessment) + practical skills assessment
- Strong emphasis on pre-use inspection, reaction force management, hose integrity, and emergency response (injection injuries)
- Medical Alert Cards / wallet cards recommended or required
- Refresher / Verification of Competency every 2–3 years
- Train-the-Trainer and audited training sites for consistency
- Clear progression: Assistant → Operator → Advanced / Supervisor → Trainer
