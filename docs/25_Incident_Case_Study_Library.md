# Section 25: Categorized Global Incident Case Study Library

**Maximum Learning Depth – Living Document**  
**Version 7.6**

This library captures high-value lessons from real HPWJ incidents (anonymised / synthesised from public industry reports, WJTA alerts, WJA lessons, and general industry knowledge). Every case is mapped back to the Master Control Register and FMEA so that training and procedures stay current.

**Purpose:** Turn past failures into permanent controls.

---

## Case 1 – Flex Lance Back-Out (Classic High RPN)
**What happened:** Operator using flex lance inside tube without properly engaged AWD. Lance ejected under pressure, striking the operator.
**Root causes:** Missing/incorrect AWD, inadequate pre-use check, production pressure.
**MCR Links:** MCR-019, MCR-020, MCR-023 (RPN 200).
**Key Lesson:** AWD is not optional. Verify engagement every single insertion. Never bypass.

## Case 2 – Hose Burst / Whip Injury
**What happened:** Aged hose that had passed visual inspection but failed under pressure; whip caused serious injury.
**Root causes:** Exceeded service life, missing annual pressure test, inadequate whip check.
**MCR Links:** MCR-001, MCR-002, MCR-006.
**Key Lesson:** Visual inspection alone is insufficient. Enforce 2/4-year life + annual proof test + whip checks both sides.

## Case 3 – Quick-Connect Disconnection Under Pressure
**What happened:** Quick-connect rotated and disconnected while hose was moving; high-pressure release and whip.
**Root causes:** Worn latch, use of quick-connect in high-movement application.
**MCR Links:** MCR-007 (RPN 180).
**Key Lesson:** Prefer screw-type or face-seal for dynamic connections. Inspect latches every time.

## Case 4 – Inadequate Pre-Use Inspection Leading to Rupture Disc Event
**What happened:** Fired rupture disc not replaced; subsequent overpressure event.
**Root causes:** Incomplete pre-use inspection, no spare disc, production urgency.
**MCR Links:** MCR-009, MCR-023 (RPN 210).
**Key Lesson:** Highest RPN item. Supervisor verification of pre-use is mandatory.

## Case 5 – Reaction Force Loss of Control
**What happened:** Handheld gun at high pressure/flow; operator lost stance and jet crossed body.
**Root causes:** Exceeded 250 N / body-weight limit, poor barrel geometry, fatigue.
**MCR Links:** MCR-016 (RPN 180).
**Key Lesson:** Three additive controls are absolute. Redesign tooling if any one fails.

## Case 6 – SIMOPS / Hot Work Interface
**What happened:** HPWJ mist contacted hot work area, contributing to fire/explosion risk (near-miss).
**Root causes:** Inadequate SIMOPS coordination and zoning.
**MCR Links:** MCR-028 (RPN 200).
**Key Lesson:** Daily SIMOPS meeting and physical separation are non-negotiable when interfaces exist.

## Case 7 – UHP Creeping Hose Uncontrolled Back-Out
**What happened:** 40k psi creeping hose ejected due to incomplete AWD and high energy.
**Root causes:** Level 2 operator used on UHP task, AWD not verified, inadequate exclusion zone.
**MCR Links:** MCR-034, MCR-019/020, MCR-031.
**Key Lesson:** UHP work is Level 3 only. Expanded controls and vacuum system required.

- Map every case to specific MCR items and training modules.

**This library will grow. Add real (anonymised) Anabeeb lessons as they occur.**
