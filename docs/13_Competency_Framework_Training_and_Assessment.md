# Chapter 13: Competency Framework, Training & Assessment

**Version 8.2**  
**Status:** Living reference – all claims mapped to Master Control Register and dual SABIC + Anabeeb sources. No self-grading language.

This chapter defines Anabeeb’s competency framework for High Pressure Water Jetting. It synthesises the strongest elements of WJTA, WJA, AS/NZS 4233 and SIR and hard-wires them to the Master Control Register (52 rows), Chapter 10 trauma protocols, OPS-P-019, SABIC OMS 8.2, and the Anabeeb Training Center (ATC).

**Guiding Principle**: Competence is not a certificate. Competence is the consistent, verifiable ability to prevent injection injuries, apply every Master Control Register control under real conditions, and lead or support a safe, high-quality job.

All training and assessment **must** map directly to the Master Control Register (especially high-priority items MCR-016, MCR-019/020, MCR-023, MCR-025, MCR-028, MCR-030, MCR-047–052) and Chapter 10.

---

## 13.1 Purpose, Scope & Success Criteria

### Purpose
Eliminate preventable HPWJ injuries and quality failures through a rigorous, auditable, multi-layered competency system.

### Scope
Covers every person who:
- Operates, assists, or supervises HPWJ equipment
- Plans, permits, or audits HPWJ work
- Trains or assesses others
- Responds to HPWJ emergencies

### Success Metrics for Anabeeb ATC
- Zero injection injuries involving trained personnel
- 100% of operators hold current Medical Alert Card + valid VOC
- 100% of high-priority MCR items demonstrably assessed
- ATC achieves TVTC recognition and becomes net revenue positive
- Client (Aramco/SABIC/PETRO RABIGH) acceptance of Anabeeb certificates without additional testing

## 13.2 Global Competency Landscape (Reference Synthesis)

### 13.2.1 WJTA (USA) – Practical Skills Focus
Foundational Training (FT) + Field Verification (FV). Strong multi-application practical assessment. Credentials transferable via HASC LINK.

### 13.2.2 WJA (UK) – Theory + Regulatory Recognition
Mandatory Safety Awareness + practical modules + workplace evidence → digital card. Strong medical injury focus and HSE recognition.

### 13.2.3 AS/NZS 4233 + National Units (Australia)
Formal units of competency. Strictest major VOC cycle (every 2 years). Clear Class A/B distinction.

### 13.2.4 SIR (Netherlands)
High-risk process industry benchmark. Strong theory + practical + medical fitness.

### 13.2.5 Anabeeb Hybrid Model
WJA-style Safety Awareness + full MCR deep dive + WJTA-style multi-application practical Field Verification + Australian 2-year VOC + mandatory Medical Alert Card + Anabeeb-specific petrochemical / SIMOPS / OPS-P-019 controls.

## 13.4 Anabeeb 3-Day Core Operator Course (ATC-HPWJ-OP-001)

**Official Name**: Anabeeb HPWJ Operator Competency Course (ATC-HPWJ-OP-001)  
**Duration**: 24–28 contact hours over 3 days  
**Class Size**: Max 8–10 for quality practical time  
**Validity**: 24 months (then VOC)  
**Pass Criteria**: Theory ≥80% + Practical Pass on **all** critical checklist items + Behavioural Pass + Medical Alert Card issued  
**Output**: Anabeeb Certificate + Photo Card + Medical Alert Card + Digital record in ATC competency register  
**MCR Controls Referenced (core)**: MCR-006, 009, 010, 016, 017, 019, 020, 023, 025, 027, 028, 030, 031, 033, 043, 047, 048, 050, 051, 052 + high-priority set.

### Detailed Daily Learning Outcomes & Content

**Day 1 – Foundations, Hazards & Controls (Theory + Workshops)**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Explain the physics of high-pressure jets and why injection injuries are catastrophic (Sec16 + Ch10 + Ch24)  
- Identify every high-priority MCR item and why it exists  
- Perform a complete pre-use inspection (MCR-023) to standard  
- Calculate and manage reaction force using the three additive controls (MCR-016). **Trainers must pull the formula constant and units from MCR-017 by reference – never hard-code the constant in slides or handouts**  
- Demonstrate correct Medical Alert Card use and emergency communication script (Ch10)  
- Apply stop-work authority correctly  
- State Anabeeb-specific team size, exclusion and rupture-disc rules (MCR-047, 048, 051)

Content: Physics & injury mechanisms deep dive, full Master Control Register workshop (including new rows 046–052), pre-use inspection practical stations, reaction force calculation exercises (always cite MCR-017), PPE & barricading (6 m SABIC baseline / 10 m Anabeeb unauthorised), permits & LOTO overview, medical emergency drills, case studies from Section 25.

**Day 2 – Equipment Mastery & Hands-On Skills**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Select correct pump configuration (flow vs pressure, plunger sizing)  
- Inspect, install, and manage hoses, fittings, whip checks, and quick-connects to MCR standard  
- Correctly use flex lances with AWD (MCR-019/020) and confirm lance tip mark ≥600 mm (MCR-052)  
- Set up and operate shotgun within MCR-050 limits (≤10 000 psi / orifice ≤1.6 mm / reaction force ≤250 N)  
- Demonstrate correct communication and team roles (including dedicated emergency-stop person – MCR-047)  
- Verify rupture disc ≤1.2× lowest MAWP and tagged (MCR-048)

Content: Pump power end/fluid end, NPSH & cavitation, hose life management & testing, whip check selection (WLL), nozzle wear & pin gauges, full practical stations (setup, pre-use, shotgunning within MCR-050, flex lancing with AWD + tip mark, emergency dump), automated system introduction (hierarchy of methods MCR-039).

**Day 3 – Integration, Field Verification & Emergency Mastery**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Plan and control a simple SIMOPS interface (MCR-028)  
- Lead or participate in a full emergency response drill for injection injury  
- Pass comprehensive practical competency assessment against the official checklist (Field Verification block)  
- Pass theory examination  
- Demonstrate safety leadership behaviours  
- Confirm 10 m unauthorised exclusion and correct team composition in a live scenario

Content: SIMOPS & communication protocols, UHP creeping hose awareness, **Field Verification block** (full practical assessment of all critical items – auditable event), emergency scenario drills (multiple), written examination, debrief, certificate & Medical Alert Card issuance, personal development planning.

### Official Practical Competency Checklist (Critical Items – Must Pass All)

These 15 items are scored pass/fail during the Day 3 Field Verification block. All must be demonstrated correctly.

1. Complete pre-use inspection of pump, hoses, fittings, nozzles, relief systems (MCR-023)
2. Correct installation and verification of whip checks / anti-whip devices (MCR-006)
3. Correct reaction force management demonstration – calculation performed using formula and constant taken from MCR-017 (MCR-016)
4. Correct flex lance AWD installation and use (MCR-019/020)
5. Lance tip clearly marked no closer than 600 mm from nozzle (MCR-052)
6. Correct emergency dump / pressure relief under time pressure (MCR-010)
7. Clear radio/hand signal communication with pump operator; no blind spots (MCR-025)
8. Correct Medical Alert Card presentation and injury communication script (MCR-043 + Ch10)
9. Stop-work authority exercise (MCR-038)
10. Hose handling & bend radius compliance (MCR-005)
11. Basic automated system safety checks / hierarchy of methods awareness (MCR-021 / 039)
12. Team composition verified: minimum three competent persons with dedicated emergency-stop role when line of sight lost (MCR-047)
13. Rupture disc correctly rated ≤1.2 × MAWP of lowest component, installed and tagged; spare available (MCR-048 / 009)
14. Shotgunning (if performed) stays within ≤10 000 psi, orifice ≤1.6 mm and reaction force ≤250 N (MCR-050)
15. 10 m unauthorised-person exclusion confirmed and enforced; system depressurised on breach (MCR-051)

(Full detailed checklist with scoring rubrics to be maintained as a controlled form in the ATC Quality System. This list is the auditable Field Verification instrument.)

### Theory Examination Structure
- 40–50 questions (multiple choice + short answer + scenario)
- Minimum 30% of marks on Master Control Register high-priority items (including 047–052)
- Minimum 20% on injection injury recognition & response (Ch10 / Ch24)
- Pass mark: 80%

## 13.6 How We Build the Training Pack (Reusable Standard for All Courses)

Every training product (3-day Basic, Supervisor, 1-day method attachments, Awareness, VOC) will be built and maintained using the same disciplined pack structure. This prevents drift and makes future consistency audits straightforward.

### Standard Training Pack Contents (per course)

1. **Course Specification** (this chapter or a dedicated file)  
   - Code, duration, entry gate, pass criteria, MCR Controls Referenced line at top

2. **Trainer Guide / Script**  
   - Day-by-day timing  
   - Exact learning outcomes  
   - Where to pull formulas/constants (always by MCR reference, never hard-coded)  
   - Sample toolbox / verification scripts (Ch12.7 style)

3. **Student Workbook**  
   - Selected Encyclopedia extracts  
   - Never Rules one-pager  
   - Reaction-force calculation sheet that cites MCR-017  
   - Medical Alert Card template  
   - Pre-Use Checklist  
   - Key tables (exclusion zones, PPE matrix, team roles)

4. **Practical Competency Checklist** (controlled form)  
   - Numbered critical items mapped to MCR IDs  
   - Pass/fail scoring only on critical items  
   - Used as the Field Verification instrument

5. **Theory Exam Bank**  
   - Mapped to MCR high-priority items + Ch10  
   - Version-controlled

6. **Assessment Record & Certificate Templates**  
   - Photo card, endorsement stamp fields, Medical Alert issue log

7. **Equipment & PPE List for Delivery**  
   - Real pump skid, AWD, hoses, suits, etc. required for that course

### Build Rules (Apply to Every Future Course)
- One course at a time (atomic).
- Every file starts with **MCR Controls Referenced** line.
- Formulas and numeric thresholds are never restated; they cite the live MCR row.
- Checklist items are the only things that create pass/fail – narrative mentions are not enough.
- Endorsements for method days go on the existing card; no new certificate types unless Level changes.
- After any MCR change, affected training packs are reviewed within 30 days (Living Document Rule).

This pack structure is what we will use next for Supervisor and then for each 1-day method attachment.

## 13.7 Assessment, Records, VOC & Continuous Competence

### Assessment Methods
- Theory examination
- Practical skills demonstration against the official checklist (Field Verification)
- Behavioural observation (safety leadership, stop-work)
- Scenario-based emergency drills
- Optional workplace logbook evidence for higher levels

### Verification of Competency (VOC) Triggers
- Every 24 months (hard requirement)
- After any lost-time or high-potential incident
- After introduction of new high-risk equipment or process
- After absence from HPWJ work >6 months
- At client request or after quality non-conformance

### Records
- Digital competency register (name, photo, level, issue/expiry dates, assessment results, Medical Alert confirmation, endorsement stamps)
- Hard-copy certificates and cards
- Retention: minimum 5 years or as required by TVTC / clients
- Audit trail for every assessment

### Continuous Professional Development (CPD)
- Minimum 8 hours structured CPD per year for Level 2+
- Toolbox talks, incident lessons, manufacturer updates, MCR changes all count

## 13.9 Medical Fitness, Human Factors & KSA-Specific Considerations

- Pre-employment and periodic medical fitness assessment recommended for all operators (vision, hearing, cardiovascular, musculoskeletal).
- Fatigue and heat-stress management critical (Ch8 + MCR-033 + Safetech Air Comfort).
- Multilingual workforce: Core materials available in English + Arabic; key safety messages and Medical Alert Card bilingual.
- Cultural factors: Strong emphasis on stop-work authority and speaking up in hierarchical environments.
- Heat stress: Explicit inclusion in training for Jubail summer conditions.

## 13.12 Never Rules – Absolute

1. Never allow any person to operate HPWJ equipment unsupervised without current Level 2+ competency and valid Medical Alert Card.  
2. Never issue a certificate without documented practical pass on every critical checklist item.  
3. Never allow a trainer to deliver the course without annual audit.  
4. Never treat VOC as a paper exercise – practical demonstration is mandatory.  
5. Never omit Master Control Register high-priority items or Chapter 10 trauma content from any course.  
6. Never hard-code reaction-force constants or other MCR numbers in training materials – always cite the live MCR row.
