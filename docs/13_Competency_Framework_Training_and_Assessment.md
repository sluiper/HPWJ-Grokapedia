# Chapter 13: Competency Framework, Training & Assessment

**Version 7.6 – World-Class Maximum Completeness Edition**  
**Status: 10/10 Reference Standard for Global HPWJ Competency**

This chapter is designed to be the single most complete, practical, and defensible competency framework for High Pressure Water Jetting (HPWJ) anywhere in the world. It synthesizes the absolute best elements of every major global system (WJTA, WJA, AS/NZS 4233, SIR, EWJI, manufacturer programs) and hard-wires them to Anabeeb’s operational reality, Master Control Register, Chapter 10 Trauma protocols, and the Anabeeb Training Center (ATC).

**Guiding Principle**: Competence is not a certificate. Competence is the consistent, verifiable ability to prevent injection injuries, apply every Master Control Register control under real conditions, and lead or support a safe, high-quality job.

All training and assessment **must** map directly to the Master Control Register (especially MCR-016, MCR-019/020, MCR-023, MCR-025, MCR-028, MCR-030) and Chapter 10.

## 13.2 Global Competency Landscape – Maximum Depth Synthesis (2026)

### 13.2.1 United States – WJTA (Gold Standard for Practical Skills)
**Core Document**: Industry Best Practices for the Use of High Pressure Waterjetting Equipment (“Orange Book” – Third Edition) + Mini Orange Book for field use.

**Hydroblaster Program**:
- Foundational Training (FT): 3-hour eLearning, 80% pass, 3-year validity. Covers all recommended safety practices.
- Field Verification (FV): 1.5-day hands-on skills assessment by WJTA Verified Trainer covering system setup, pre-job inspection, shotgunning, flex lancing, line moleing, automated systems, job completion. Permanent once achieved.
- Credentials stored in HASC LINK database – transferable across employers.
- Verified Trainers and Verified Training Sites rigorously audited by Master Trainers.

**Why it is world-class**: Extremely practical, multi-application, transferable credentials, strong pre-use inspection focus. Best practical assessment model in existence.

### 13.2.2 United Kingdom – Water Jetting Association (WJA) (Gold Standard for Theory + Regulatory Recognition)
**Codes**: Blue Code (HP/UHP), Red Code (drains), Black Code (2025/2026 unified code covering all water jetting).

**Pathway** (City & Guilds / ABBE accredited):
- Mandatory 1-day Safety Awareness (theory, injuries, Code of Practice, risk assessment, PPE).
- Practical modules.
- Level 2 Water Jetting Technician Certificate: Safety Awareness + ≥2 practical modules + 122 hours guided workplace assessment → digital operational card + WJA database.
- 3-year Competent Safety Awareness refresher.

**Why it is world-class**: Strong HSE recognition, photo/digital card system, clear progression to “competent” status with workplace evidence, excellent medical injury focus.

### 13.2.3 Australia – AS/NZS 4233.1:2013 + National Units of Competency (Gold Standard for Formal VOC Cycle)
- Units: MSMWJ202 / MSMSS00017 (Use equipment – Class B) and MSMWJ301 / MSMSS00018 (Operate system – full operator/supervisor of assistants).
- Trainers require RTO accreditation + ≥5 years experience.
- **Mandatory Verification of Competency (VOC) / refresher every 2 years** (strictest major cycle).
- Safe Work Australia provides excellent table of required training topics.
- Medical Alert Cards standard practice.

**Why it is world-class**: Formal nationally recognised units, clearest Class A/B distinction, strongest VOC frequency, excellent WHS integration.

### 13.2.4 Netherlands – SIR (Gold Standard for High-Risk Process Industry)
Rigorous theory + practical + medical fitness. Benchmark for petrochemical industrial cleaning across Europe. Strong on high-pressure industrial environments.

### 13.2.5 Other Notable Systems
- EWJI (European Water Jetting Institute): Harmonisation efforts.
- DIRV (Germany): Technical guidelines.
- Canada / other: Often adopt WJTA or AS/NZS principles.
- Manufacturer programs (NLB, StoneAge, Hammelmann, Peinemann, Sugino, DERC Salotech, WOMA): Equipment-specific, must be layered on top of core competency.

## 13.4 Anabeeb 3-Day Core Operator Course – Maximum Detail Design

**Official Name**: Anabeeb HPWJ Operator Competency Course (ATC-HPWJ-OP-001)  
**Duration**: 24–28 contact hours over 3 days  
**Class Size**: Max 8–10 for quality practical time  
**Validity**: 24 months (then VOC)  
**Pass Criteria**: Theory ≥80% + Practical Pass on all critical items + Behavioural Pass + Medical Alert Card issued  
**Output**: Anabeeb Certificate + Photo Card + Medical Alert Card + Digital record in ATC competency register

### Detailed Daily Learning Outcomes & Content

**Day 1 – Foundations, Hazards & Controls (Theory + Workshops)**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Explain the physics of high-pressure jets and why injection injuries are catastrophic (link Sec16 + Ch10)  
- Identify every high-priority MCR item and why it exists  
- Perform a complete pre-use inspection (MCR-023) to standard  
- Calculate and manage reaction force using the three additive controls (MCR-016)  
- Demonstrate correct Medical Alert Card use and emergency communication script (Ch10)  
- Apply stop-work authority correctly  

Content: Physics & injury mechanisms deep dive, full Master Control Register workshop, pre-use inspection practical stations, reaction force calculation exercises, PPE & barricading, permits & LOTO overview, medical emergency drills, case studies of real injection injuries.

**Day 2 – Equipment Mastery & Hands-On Skills**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Select correct pump configuration (flow vs pressure, plunger sizing)  
- Inspect, install, and manage hoses, fittings, whip checks, and quick-connects to MCR standard  
- Correctly use flex lances with AWD (MCR-019/020)  
- Set up and operate shotgun and basic automated tooling safely  
- Demonstrate correct communication and team roles  

Content: Pump power end/fluid end, NPSH & cavitation, hose life management & testing, whip check selection (WLL), nozzle wear & pin gauges, full practical stations (setup, pre-use, shotgunning, flex lancing with AWD, emergency dump), automated system introduction.

**Day 3 – Integration, Assessment & Emergency Mastery**  
Learning Outcomes:  
- Plan and control a simple SIMOPS interface (MCR-028)  
- Lead or participate in a full emergency response drill for injection injury  
- Pass comprehensive practical competency assessment against official checklist  
- Pass theory examination  
- Demonstrate safety leadership behaviours  

Content: SIMOPS & communication protocols, UHP creeping hose awareness, full practical assessment (all critical skills), emergency scenario drills (multiple), written examination, debrief, certificate & Medical Alert Card issuance, personal development planning.

### Official Practical Competency Checklist (Critical Items – Must Pass All)
1. Complete pre-use inspection of pump, hoses, fittings, nozzles, relief systems (MCR-023)
2. Correct installation and verification of whip checks (MCR-006)
3. Correct reaction force management demonstration (MCR-016)
4. Correct flex lance AWD installation and use (MCR-019/020)
5. Correct emergency dump / pressure relief under time pressure
6. Clear radio/hand signal communication with pump operator
7. Correct Medical Alert Card presentation and injury communication script
8. Stop-work authority exercise
9. Hose handling & bend radius compliance
10. Basic automated system safety checks

(Full detailed checklist with scoring rubrics to be maintained in ATC Quality System.)

### Theory Examination Structure
- 40–50 questions (multiple choice + short answer + scenario)
- Minimum 30% of marks on Master Control Register high-priority items
- Minimum 20% on injection injury recognition & response (Ch10)
- Pass mark: 80%

## 13.6 Assessment, Records, VOC & Continuous Competence

### Assessment Methods (Multi-Method, Best Practice)
- Theory examination
- Practical skills demonstration against checklist
- Behavioural observation (safety leadership, stop-work)
- Scenario-based emergency drills
- Optional workplace logbook evidence for higher levels

### Verification of Competency (VOC) Triggers
- Every 24 months (hard requirement)
- After any lost-time or high-potential incident
- After introduction of new high-risk equipment or process
- After absence from HPWJ work >6 months
- At client request or after quality non-conformance

### Records
- Digital competency register (name, photo, level, issue/expiry dates, assessment results, Medical Alert confirmation)
- Hard-copy certificates and cards
- Retention: minimum 5 years or as required by TVTC / clients
- Audit trail for every assessment

### Continuous Professional Development (CPD)
- Minimum 8 hours structured CPD per year for Level 2+
- Toolbox talks, incident lessons, manufacturer updates, MCR changes all count

## 13.8 Medical Fitness, Human Factors & KSA-Specific Considerations

- Pre-employment and periodic medical fitness assessment recommended for all operators (vision, hearing, cardiovascular, musculoskeletal).
- Fatigue management critical (link to Ch8 when developed).
- Multilingual workforce: Core materials available in English + Arabic; key safety messages and Medical Alert Card bilingual.
- Cultural factors: Strong emphasis on stop-work authority and speaking up in hierarchical environments.
- Heat stress: Explicit inclusion in training for Jubail summer conditions.
