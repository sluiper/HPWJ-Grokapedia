# Chapter 13: Competency Framework, Training & Assessment

**Maximum Global Depth + Anabeeb ATC Alignment**

This chapter establishes the definitive competency framework for High Pressure Water Jetting (HPWJ) personnel at Anabeeb. It integrates the best available global standards (WJTA, WJA, AS/NZS 4233, SIR, EWJI, manufacturer programs) with KSA/TVTC requirements, ECITB pathways, and the practical needs of the Anabeeb Training Center (ATC).

All training, assessment, and competence management **must** reference the Master Control Register (MCR) and Chapter 10 (Emergency Response & Trauma).

---

## 13.1 Purpose and Scope

To ensure every person who operates, supervises, or supports HPWJ operations is demonstrably competent to:
- Prevent high-pressure injection injuries and other severe incidents
- Apply all MCR controls correctly
- Respond effectively in emergencies (Ch10)
- Deliver safe, efficient, and consistent work quality

This framework covers:
- Competency levels and role definitions
- Initial training pathways
- Refresher and continuous development
- Assessment methods (theory + practical + observation)
- Certification, records, and verification of competency (VOC)
- Trainer and assessor standards
- Anabeeb 3-day core course and specialty modules

**Linked MCR Items**: MCR-016 (Reaction Force), MCR-019/020 (Flex Lance AWD), MCR-023 (Pre-Use Inspection), MCR-025 (Communication), MCR-028 (SIMOPS), MCR-030 (Training Link).

## 13.2 Global Competency Landscape – Deep Dive

HPWJ training is not uniform worldwide. Below is a synthesis of the leading frameworks (current as of 2026).

### 13.2.1 United States – WJTA (WaterJet Technology Association)
- **Core Document**: Industry Best Practices for the Use of High Pressure Waterjetting Equipment ("Orange Book" – Third Edition)
- **Hydroblaster Training & Certification Program**:
  - **Foundational Training (FT)**: 3-hour eLearning, 80% pass, valid 3 years. Covers recommended safety practices.
  - **Field Verification (FV)**: 1.5-day hands-on with WJTA Verified Trainer. Covers system setup, pre-job inspection, shotgunning, flex lancing, line moleing, manual & automated methods. No expiration once achieved.
  - Credentials stored in HASC LINK database, transferable between employers.
  - Verified Trainers and Verified Training Sites audited by Master Trainers.
- **Strength**: Highly practical, industry-driven, strong focus on pre-use inspection and equipment-specific skills. Excellent model for Anabeeb ATC practical assessments.

### 13.2.2 United Kingdom – Water Jetting Association (WJA)
- **Codes of Practice**:
  - Blue Code: High & Ultra-High Pressure water jetting
  - Red Code: Drain/sewer jetting
  - Black Code (2025/2026 unification): Single code covering all water jetting types (pressure washing to UHP)
- **Training Pathway** (City & Guilds accredited):
  - **Water Jetting Safety Awareness**: Mandatory 1-day classroom (theory, hazards, PPE, injuries, risk assessment). Based on the Code of Practice. Every operative must complete this.
  - Practical modules (Surface Preparation, Drain Cleaning, etc.)
  - **Level 2 Water Jetting Technician Certificate**: Safety Awareness + at least 2 practical modules + 122 hours guided workplace assessment by WJA assessor. Leads to digital operational jetting card and database entry.
  - Refresher: Competent Safety Awareness every 3 years.
- **Strength**: Strong regulatory recognition (HSE), photo card system, clear progression to competent status. Medical alert emphasis.

### 13.2.3 Australia – AS/NZS 4233 + National Units of Competency
- **Standard**: AS/NZS 4233.1:2013 High pressure water jetting systems – Safe operation and maintenance (and Part 2 Construction/Performance)
- **Units**:
  - MSMWJ202 / MSMSS00017: Use high pressure water jetting equipment (Class B assistant / basic operator)
  - MSMWJ301 / MSMSS00018: Operate a high pressure water jetting system (full operator / supervisor of assistants)
- **Requirements**: Training via Registered Training Organisation (RTO). Trainers need ≥5 years experience.
- **Refresher / VOC**: Every 2 years mandatory.
- **Medical Alert Cards**: Explicitly recommended / required by many providers.
- **Strength**: Nationally recognised units of competency, clear Class A/B distinction, strong WHS integration. Safe Work Australia guidance table of training topics is excellent reference for course design.

### 13.2.4 Netherlands – SIR (Stichting Industriële Reiniging)
- Industry benchmark for industrial cleaning / water jetting in the Netherlands and increasingly Europe.
- Rigorous certification with theory + practical + medical fitness elements.
- Strong focus on high-risk industrial environments (petrochemical).

### 13.2.5 Europe / Other
- **EWJI** (European Water Jetting Institute): Developing harmonised standards.
- **DIRV (Germany)**: National association with technical guidelines.
- **Manufacturer programs**: NLB, StoneAge, Hammelmann, Peinemann, WOMA, DERC Salotech all offer equipment-specific training that should be layered on top of core competency.

### 13.2.6 Summary Comparison Table

| Framework | Core Theory | Practical | Validity / Refresher | Medical Alert | Assessment Style | Best For |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|----------------------|---------------|------------------|----------|
| WJTA (USA) | 3h eLearning | 1.5 days FV | FT 3 yrs; FV permanent | Strongly recommended | Skills checklist by Verified Trainer | Industrial / petrochemical |
| WJA (UK) | 1-day Safety Awareness | Modules + workplace hours | 3 years | Emphasised | City & Guilds + assessor | Comprehensive UK/EU recognition |
| AS/NZS 4233 (AU) | RTO units | Class B skill sets | 2 years VOC/refresher | Required in practice | National competency units | Formal national system |
| SIR (NL) | Full certification | High | Periodic | Yes | Industry body | High-risk process industry |

**Anabeeb Recommendation**: Hybrid model – adopt WJA-style Safety Awareness + WJTA-style practical Field Verification + Australian-style 2-year VOC + mandatory Medical Alert Card (from Ch10).

## 13.4 Anabeeb 3-Day Core Training Course (ATC Flagship)

**Target**: New Operators and existing personnel requiring full competency.

**Duration**: 3 days (theory + practical + assessment)
**Validity**: 2 years (then VOC/refresher)
**Output**: Anabeeb HPWJ Operator Certificate + Medical Alert Card + MCR awareness confirmation

### Day 1 – Foundations & Safety (Theory Heavy)
- HPWJ physics and injury mechanisms (link Sec16 + Ch10)
- Global standards overview (this chapter)
- Master Control Register deep dive (all high-priority items)
- Pre-use inspection (MCR-023) – full checklist practice
- Reaction force controls (MCR-016)
- PPE, barricading, permits, LOTO
- Medical emergency response & Medical Alert Card use (Ch10)

### Day 2 – Equipment & Practical Skills
- Pump types, flow vs pressure, plunger changes
- Hose management, whip checks, quick-connects (MCR-001 to MCR-008)
- Nozzles, flex lances, AWD (MCR-019/020)
- Hands-on setup, pre-use, shotgunning, flex lancing (under supervision)
- Automated systems introduction (Peinemann style)

### Day 3 – Advanced Operations, Assessment & Emergency Drills
- SIMOPS, communication protocols (MCR-025/028)
- UHP / creeping hose awareness
- Full practical assessment against competency checklist
- Emergency scenario drills (injection injury response)
- Written test (80% pass) + practical pass/fail
- Issue of certificates and Medical Alert Cards

**Assessment Criteria**:
- Theory: ≥80% on multi-choice + short answer (MCR-focused)
- Practical: Demonstrate all critical controls (pre-use, reaction force management, AWD use, emergency stop, communication)
- Behavioural: Safety-first attitude, stop-work authority exercise

## 13.6 Assessment, Records & Verification of Competency (VOC)

- All assessments documented against MCR items and competency checklist.
- Records retained for minimum 5 years (or as per company policy / TVTC).
- VOC: Every 2 years or after significant incident / equipment change / long absence.
- Digital / physical card system (photo, name, level, expiry, blood type reference to Medical Alert).
- Supervisor spot-checks of competence in the field.

---

## 13.7 Trainer & Assessor Standards

- Minimum: Level 4 Operator + proven field experience (≥3–5 years recommended)
- Preferred: WJTA Verified Trainer equivalent or WJA Assessor pathway
- All ATC trainers must complete internal Master Trainer development and be audited annually.
- Continuous professional development mandatory.

---

## 13.8 Integration with External Bodies (KSA Context)

- **TVTC**: ATC courses designed for potential accreditation / recognition.
- **ECITB**: Align MJI-10 and other mechanical integrity training where relevant; explore HPWJ modules if available.
- **NEBOSH**: Embed general HSE principles (risk assessment, hierarchy of control, human factors).
- **Client Requirements**: Aramco / SABIC / PETRO RABIGH specific add-ons to be layered as needed.
- **Manufacturer**: NLB, StoneAge, etc. equipment-specific training as add-on.

## 13.10 Never Rules – Competency

- Never allow untrained or expired personnel to operate HPWJ equipment unsupervised.
- Never skip Medical Alert Card issuance.
- Never treat competency as a one-time event (2-year VOC minimum).
- Never deliver training that does not explicitly cover the Master Control Register high-priority items.
