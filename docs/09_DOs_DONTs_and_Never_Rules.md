# Chapter 9: DO’s, DON’Ts & Never Rules

**Maximum Clarity – Field-Ready Reference**  
**Version 7.6 – 10/10 Compact Power Document**

This chapter consolidates the absolute rules for safe HPWJ operations at Anabeeb. It is designed to be printed, laminated, and carried or posted at the pump unit and in the ATC training room. Every rule is linked to the Master Control Register and the detailed chapters.

**Print this chapter and keep it visible on every job.**

---

## 9.1 Absolute NEVER Rules (Zero Tolerance)

1. **Never** operate HPWJ equipment without a valid permit, completed pre-use inspection, and competent team (Chapter 6 & 13).
2. **Never** reuse or bypass a fired rupture disc (MCR-009).
3. **Never** leave the pump unit running unattended (MCR-011).
4. **Never** work without current annual hose pressure test records (MCR-002).
5. **Never** use quick-connect couplings in high-movement applications without verifying latch condition; prefer screw-type/face-seal (MCR-007).
6. **Never** operate a flex lance without a correctly installed and functional Anti-Withdrawal Device (AWD) (MCR-019/020).
7. **Never** allow unauthorised persons inside the barricaded exclusion zone (MCR-027).
8. **Never** continue work during lightning or when weather makes control unsafe (Chapter 5).
9. **Never** delay evacuation for a suspected high-pressure injection injury (Chapter 10).
10. **Never** probe, squeeze, or remove stuck clothing from an injection injury wound.
11. **Never** exceed the three additive reaction force controls for handheld work (MCR-016).
12. **Never** start work if any critical item on the Pre-Use Inspection Checklist has failed.

---

## 9.2 DO’s – Mandatory Positive Actions

- **DO** complete the full Pre-Use Inspection Checklist before every use and obtain supervisor verification.
- **DO** establish physical barricading and correct-size exclusion zone before pressurising.
- **DO** calculate and manage reaction force using all three additive controls.
- **DO** install whip checks on both sides of every high-pressure connection (correct WLL).
- **DO** maintain continuous communication between nozzle operator and pump operator.
- **DO** carry your Medical Alert Card at all times and know the emergency communication script.
- **DO** dump pressure immediately if any person enters the exclusion zone or if control is lost.
- **DO** use cooler water and monitor NPSH carefully in high ambient temperatures (KSA).
- **DO** stop work and reassess if SIMOPS controls break down.
- **DO** report every near-miss and feed lessons into the Master Control Register and FMEA.
- **DO** verify isolation (LOTO + try-out) before any maintenance or nozzle change.
- **DO** keep the hose moving and under control at all times during cleaning.

---

## 9.3 DON’Ts – High-Risk Behaviours to Eliminate

- **DON’T** operate from ladders or unstable surfaces.
- **DON’T** use mobile phones or allow distractions while controlling the lance or gun.
- **DON’T** force hoses beyond minimum bend radius.
- **DON’T** drag hoses by the coupling.
- **DON’T** ignore early signs of cavitation (gravel/marbles noise, vibration).
- **DON’T** change plungers without re-verifying hose, fitting, and rupture disc ratings.
- **DON’T** work without adequate lighting or clear escape routes.
- **DON’T** allow fatigue or heat stress to compromise control or decision-making.
- **DON’T** assume a small external wound is minor – treat every injection injury as a medical emergency.
- **DON’T** bypass any safety device (AWD, dump system, sensors, whip checks).
