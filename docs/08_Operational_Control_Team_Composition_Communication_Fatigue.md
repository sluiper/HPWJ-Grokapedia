# Chapter 8: Operational Control, Team Composition, Communication & Fatigue Management

**Version 8.1 – Full Depth (SABIC OMS 8.2 Integrated)**  
**Primary Sources:** SABIC OMS Attachment 8.2 (High Pressure Water Jetting – Mandatory for KSA / MEA), YANSAB JSA & Pre-Job Checklist (SHEM-aligned), SABIC HPWJ Awareness PPT (SHEM 08.07), Master Control Register, Section 26.

## 8.1 Hierarchy of Methods – Operational Control Starts with Selection (SABIC 2.1)

SABIC OMS 8.2 Table 4.1 is the mandatory risk-based selection tool. Operational control begins **before** the pump is started by choosing the lowest-risk method that is reasonably practicable.

| Method | Personnel Exposure | Permission Status (SABIC) | Operational Control Implication |
|--------|--------------------|---------------------------|---------------------------------|
| Fully automated mechanized, controls outside jetting area | None inside | Yes | Preferred. Zero line-of-fire for operators. |
| Mechanized, controls outside (>6 m or physical shielding) | None inside | Yes | Preferred. |
| Mechanized, controls inside jetting area | Inside | Yes with team JSA | Full PPE + continuous supervision. |
| Manual cleaning with strain relief (no reaction force) inside | Inside | Yes with team JSA | Full PPE + reaction-force zero. |
| Manual cleaning without strain relief (exposed to reaction force) | Inside | **Discouraged** – Area Sr. Manager approval + RA | Highest human-factor load. MCR-016 three additive controls absolute. |
| Manual flexible lance with hose catcher / AWD | Inside | Only rare cases – **Director** approval + RA | MCR-019 / 020 absolute. Level 3 only. |
| Manual flexible lance without catcher / open-ended hose | Inside | **Not permitted** | Absolute Never Rule (MCR-038). |

**Operational rule (MCR-039):** Automated or mechanized methods with operators outside the jetting area shall be selected wherever reasonably practicable. Manual reaction-force work requires documented justification and elevated approval. Flexible lance without AWD is forbidden.

## 8.2 Team Composition & Roles (SABIC 2.7 + 2.3)

### 8.2.1 Minimum Team Size – Absolute

- **Minimum two competent persons** for any pressurised work (SABIC 2.7.3, Checklist item 26).
- **Lone working is prohibited** (SABIC 2.2.4).
- One person operates the lance/gun (primary duty: direct the jet).
- Second person attends the pump unit, watches the lance operator for difficulty/fatigue, and monitors the surrounding area for intrusion or unsafe conditions.

### 8.2.2 When Additional Members Are Required (SABIC 2.7.4)

| Situation | Extra Role |
|-----------|------------|
| Dump valve (dead-man) is remote from the jet operator | Dedicated dump operator |
| Fully sheeted enclosure | Extra person for communication / emergency |
| Complex SIMOPS or multi-gun | SIMOPS coordinator + additional watchers |
| UHP creeping hose / tube bundle | Level 3 operator + vacuum operator + support (MCR-034) |
| Confined space + HPWJ | Full confined-space team + rescue plan + HPWJ-specific controls |

### 8.2.3 Named Roles & Accountabilities

| Role | Who | Core Duties (SABIC) |
|------|-----|---------------------|
| **HPWJ Authorising Person** | Appointed by End-User Manager (e.g. Site Maintenance Manager) | Reviews & accepts Technical Specification (ATT-1); ensures contractor is from SABIC GPS approved list; ensures training/certification; leads JSA; supervises; periodic site visits; accepts that controls are in place before permit issue (MCR-041). |
| **HPWJ Supervisor** (Contractor) | Trained & competent; may be one of the operators | Primary liaison with Work Control Permit Issuer; ensures pre-use checks (ATT-6); accepts the HPWJ work permit; controls the team; stops work on any deviation (MCR-036). |
| **Lance / Gun Operator** | Level-appropriate competency | Controls jet direction and has final authority over pressure relief. Leads while jetting is in progress. |
| **Pump Operator** | Level-appropriate competency | Controls pump; never increases pressure without clear instruction from lance operator; monitors for fatigue/intrusion. |
| **All Crew** | Current TQCP (or equivalent) + Medical Alert Card | Stop-work authority; carry Medical Alert Card; know hand signals and dump command. |

**Rotation:** Team members may (and should) rotate duties during the job to minimise fatigue (SABIC 2.7.6).

## 8.3 Competency Gate (SABIC 2.3.4 – Operational Control Prerequisite)

Before any person is allowed on an HPWJ team:

1. Third-party certification & qualification from **TQCP or equivalent** for the **specific method/technique and pressure class** being used (e.g. automatic machine qualification does **not** authorise manual HPWJ).
2. Minimum **one year experience** in similar work.
3. Certification refreshed on a **two-year** basis.
4. Stamped in third-party qualification passport.
5. Medical fitness confirmed.
6. Current Medical Alert Card in possession (SABIC 2.3.7, Checklist item 31).
7. EHSS orientation + HPWJ Hazard Awareness (ATT-3) completed.

**MCR-042:** No person without current method-specific third-party certification (TQCP or equivalent), 1-year relevant experience, and 2-year refresh may perform or supervise HPWJ. Supervisor verifies before every job (MCR-030 / MCR-036).

## 8.4 Communication Protocol (SABIC 2.7.2 + Checklist 29–30)

### 8.4.1 Absolute Requirements

- Communication methods shall be **agreed before work starts**.
- The team must be able to pass messages between the lance holder and the pump operator **without moving around corners or blind spots**.
- **Line of sight or other continuous communication** between lance/gun operator and pump operator is mandatory.
- Hand signals shall be defined and communicated to the entire crew.
- Any breakdown in communication = **immediate dump and stop** (MCR-025).
- No mobile phone use while controlling lance or pump.

### 8.4.2 Standard Hand Signals (Baseline – adapt and brief)

| Signal | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Thumbs up / OK | Ready / pressure OK |
| Hand across throat | Dump / kill pressure immediately |
| Arms crossed above head | Emergency stop – everyone |
| Pointing to eyes then to location | Look / hazard there |
| Fist raised | Hold / do not increase pressure |

**Pre-job briefing (MCR-025):** Every team member, including the Supervisor, endorses the JSA and Technical Specification to demonstrate understanding. Changes or deviations during the job require immediate stop and re-notification of Permit Issuer and Authorising Person (SABIC 2.5.4).

## 8.5 Fatigue, Heat Stress & Human Factors (SABIC + MCR-033 + Section 26)

Full PPE (especially 20/30 or TurtleSkin WaterArmor) plus high ambient temperatures in Jubail create extreme thermal and physical load.

### 8.5.1 Mandatory Controls

- **Work/rest regimen** must be followed (SABIC 2.4.5.4).
- **Worker rotation** technique implemented (JSA control).
- Rest area with refreshers provided.
- Never allow a fatigued operator to continue controlling a high-energy lance.
- High-reaction-force or UHP tasks require more frequent rotation.
- Monitor for signs of heat stress / heat exhaustion (pale, sweaty, confusion, loss of coordination).
- Non-slip footwear mandatory; work area clear of loose items/debris.

### 8.5.2 Link to Reaction Force (MCR-016)

The three additive reaction-force controls assume the operator can maintain stance and attention. Fatigue degrades all three. Maximum longitudinal reaction force **≤ 250 N** (SABIC 2.4.5.2, YANSAB JSA, MCR-016).

**Formula (confirmed in SABIC docs and YANSAB JSA):**

```
Back Thrust (lbf) = 0.052 × Flow (GPM) × √Pressure (psi)
1 lbf = 4.448 N
```

Any planned force > 250 N requires mechanical assistance, strain-relief tooling, or elevated approval and is discouraged for pure manual work.

**MCR-033 remains primary** for heat-stress work/rest/hydration/shade. Section 26 expands the human-factors interaction.

## 8.6 Pre-Job Operational Gate – ATT-6 Checklist is Non-Negotiable

SABIC requires completion of **Attachment 6 – Pre-Job HPWJ Safety Checklist** as a **pre-requisite** to issue of the HPWJ Work Permit (ATT-7 / SHEM 08.10).

Key operational items from the checklist that the Supervisor and Authorising Person must verify:

**Site Preparation**
- Area (including far end of unit) barricaded with “Danger – Water Jetting in Progress” signs.
- Barricade minimum **6 metres** from jetting area (SABIC 2.6.11). Robust screens (≥2 m high) may allow closer (not <3 m) if agreed and documented.
- Sensitive / electrical equipment protected.
- Environmental controls (bunding, drain routing, waste disposal) confirmed with Environmental team.
- Moving equipment LOTO’d (SHEM 08.11).

**Equipment Preparation**
- Dead-man / dump tested and functional.
- Third-party inspection current; rupture disc / PSV correct.
- Hoses: no kinks/cuts/exposed braid; pressure rating engraved; annual test certificate; lifespan 4 years (flex lances 2 years).
- No quick-connects on high-pressure hoses.
- Safety shroud ≥6 ft at gun/lance connection.
- Shotgun barrel ≥48 inches (1.2 m) from trigger to tip (SABIC 2.6.5).
- Anti-withdrawal devices installed and operable for tube work.
- Hose anti-whip devices at all connections.
- System leak-tested at maximum anticipated pressure.
- Pump grounded; hazardous-area classification respected.

**Personnel Preparation**
- Minimum two trained/certified persons (method-specific).
- Scope, method, hazard awareness (ATT-3) and JSA understood and signed.
- Line-of-sight / communication established.
- Hand signals briefed.
- All crew carry Medical Alert Card.
- Emergency first-aid, egress and medical response reviewed.
- Full PPE for the pressure class (see 8.7).

Only after ATT-6 is complete and signed does the Work Permit Issuer issue the HPWJ permit.

## 8.7 PPE as Operational Control (Last Resort – SABIC 2.4.6)

PPE is the last line of defence. It does **not** replace hierarchy of methods or engineering controls.

**Pressure-class matched PPE (SABIC + TST / TurtleSkin reference):**

| Pressure Band | Body / Hand Protection | Notes |
|---------------|------------------------|-------|
| Up to 14 500 psi (≈1000 bar) | 10/28 rating or equivalent | Yellow label (TST) |
| Up to 30 000 psi (≈2000–3000 bar) | 20/30 rating or TurtleSkin WaterArmor | Orange label (TST); higher thermal load |
| Foot | Waterproof boots with steel/Kevlar toes + metatarsal guards; 3000 boots or WaterArmor for high pressure | |
| Shin | Gun operators shall wear shin guards | |
| Head / Face | Hard hat + face shield + safety glasses (goggles if aerosol). Full-face visor BS EN 166 Impact Grade B minimum + chin protection | Head protection 20/30 rating |
| Hearing | As required by noise level | |
| Gloves | Correct size; must not jam gun trigger | Specialist jet-penetration resistant recommended |

Full body protection suit for the HPWJ pressure is required when inside the jetting area. See manufacturer guidance (TST catalogue, TurtleSkin) for exact certified combinations. RPE added if process residues produce hazardous mist/aerosol (MSDS-driven).

## 8.8 Real-Time Operational Control Rules (During Jetting)

1. Only HPWJ team members are permitted inside the barriered area while jetting is in progress. Any inspection or entry requires **suspension of jetting** and escort by the HPWJ Supervisor (SABIC 2.7.9).
2. The lance operator has the **lead role** while jetting is in progress; the pump operator never increases pressure without clear instruction.
3. Continuous monitoring of hose behaviour, reaction force, exclusion-zone integrity, and operator condition.
4. Any unauthorised person entering the barricade = **immediate stop**.
5. Any malfunction, leak, abnormal noise, or communication loss = **immediate dump**.
6. Never leave equipment running or pressurised unattended.
7. Never operate from a ladder. Scaffolding must be designed/erected by competent persons and secured against being pushed over by reaction force. Full body harness + fall arrest per SHEM 08.09 when working at height.
8. Steel guarding preferred for lance operator to stand behind (especially when jetting downwards on tube bundles).
9. HPWJ Authorising Person undertakes **periodic visits** to confirm controls remain in place (SABIC 2.7.8).

## 8.9 Stop-Work Authority & Escalation

- **Any** team member can call stop-work. The call is absolute and non-negotiable (MCR-038).
- Supervisor supports the call and never allows production pressure to override safety controls (MCR-036).
- Changes or deviations → stop → notify Work Permit Issuer and HPWJ Authorising Person → re-assess / re-permit if required.
- Post-stop: full controlled shutdown sequence (Chapter 11) before any investigation or restart.

---

## 8.10 Never Rules – Operational Control (SABIC Do’s & Don’ts + MCR-038)

**Never:**
- Commence work without necessary permits and Technical Specification.
- Operate without required PPE for the pressure class.
- Run equipment with any leakage without rectifying (unit depressurised first).
- Tighten any pressure joint under pressure.
- Bypass safety cut-outs or dump systems.
- Operate guns/valves with operating lever tied, wedged or locked ON.
- Operate with badly worn or undersize nozzles.
- Continue if unauthorised personnel enter the area.
- Operate at reaction force greater than the operator can comfortably absorb (≤250 N absolute).
- Leave equipment running or unattended on site.
- Work as a single person under pressure.
- Use flexible lance without AWD / hose catcher (not permitted).
- Jet from a ladder or unstable platform.
- Ignore fatigue or heat-stress signs.

**Always (key Do’s):**
- Complete ATT-1 Technical Specification and formal JSA.
- Erect barriers + warning signs before any pressurisation.
- Test dump / dead-man every pre-use.
- Fit whip restraints / anti-whip devices and safety shrouds.
- Increase pressure slowly; re-check couplings for leaks.
- Rotate team members to minimise fatigue.
- Release all pressure on any shutdown.
- Carry Medical Alert Card and know the emergency response (Chapter 10).

## 8.11 Work Process Overview (SABIC 2.5) – Operational Control Flow

| Step | Responsibility |
|------|----------------|
| Assess alternatives; complete Technical form ATT-1 | Job requester + HPWJ Authorising Person |
| Arrange approved contractor | HPWJ Authorising Person |
| Off-site prep (equipment, personnel, PPE, inspection, training) | Contractor Supervisor |
| Formal JSA/RA (Table 4.1 basis), inspect equipment, permit request | Authorising Person + Contractor Supervisor + Safety |
| Issue permit to set up site | Work Permit Issuer |
| Set up site | Contractor + Safety, agreed by Authorising Person & Area Owner |
| Complete ATT-6 Pre-Job Checklist → Issue HPWJ Work Permit | Permit Issuer + Authorising Person + Contractor Supervisor |
| Perform work under continuous control | Contractor team; Authorising Person supervises |
| Remove equipment, restore site | Contractor |
| Return & sign-off permit | Contractor + Safety, agreed by Authorising Person & Area Owner |

This sequence is mandatory for KSA sites.

## 8.12 Link to Medical Emergency (Chapter 10)

Because HPWJ injection injuries appear minor externally but destroy deep tissue, **every** crew member carries a waterproof Medical Alert Card (SABIC 2.3.7). The card and the communication script in Chapter 10 ensure receiving clinicians understand the mechanism. Time is tissue. Operational control includes knowing the nearest capable surgical facility and the exact wording to use on the radio.
