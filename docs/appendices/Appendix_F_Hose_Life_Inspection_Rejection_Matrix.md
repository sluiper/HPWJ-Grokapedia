# Appendix F: Hose Life, Inspection & Rejection Matrix

**Status:** Draft on `draft/appendices`  
**Source:** MCR-001 to MCR-008 + Section 18 + existing Whip Check card  
**Purpose:** One-page field matrix for hose inspection decisions. Print and keep with the hose records.

---

## F.1 Maximum Service Life (MCR-001)

| Hose Type | Maximum Life from Date of Manufacture or Receipt | Notes |
|-----------|--------------------------------------------------|-------|
| Flex lances & shotgun whip hoses | **2 years** | Absolute – retire even if visually perfect |
| All other high-pressure hoses | **4 years** | Absolute – retire even if visually perfect |

Life starts from the earlier of manufacture date or receipt date. No extensions without formal engineering justification and QHSSE approval.

---

## F.2 Mandatory Testing (MCR-002)

- Annual third-party pressure / proof testing is mandatory in addition to visual inspection.
- ~7% of hoses that pass visual inspection still fail pressure test (industry data referenced in Section 18).
- Records must be available on site or immediately accessible.

---

## F.3 Colour Coding & Marking (MCR-003)

- Permanent MAWP marking + colour coding must be visible from 2 ft and matched to system pressure.
- Missing or illegible marking = reject.

---

## F.4 Immediate Reject Criteria (MCR-004) – Any One = Remove from Service

| Defect | Action |
|--------|--------|
| Exposed wire braid | **Reject** |
| Bulging or blistering | **Reject** |
| Cuts, gouges or abrasions that reach the reinforcement | **Reject** |
| Permanent kinks or flattened sections | **Reject** |
| Weepage or leakage under pressure or at rest | **Reject** |
| Damaged, cracked or corroded fittings / end connections | **Reject** |
| Exceeded service life (2 yr flex / 4 yr standard) | **Reject** |
| Missing or illegible MAWP / colour coding | **Reject** |
| Failed annual pressure test | **Reject** |

---

## F.5 Handling Rules (MCR-005)

- Never exceed minimum bend radius.
- Support the weight of the hose; never drag by the coupling.
- Protect from vehicle traffic, sharp edges, and hot surfaces.

---

## F.6 Whip Checks (MCR-006)

- Required on **both sides** of every high-pressure connection.
- Correct Working Load Limit (WLL) for the application.
- Inspect every use; replace if frayed, damaged or incorrectly fitted.
- Prefer HPWJ-rated whip checks.

---

## F.7 Couplings (MCR-007 / MCR-008)

- Prefer screw-type or face-seal couplings wherever relative movement or rotation is possible.
- Quick-connects: inspect latches/pins every use; never use where rotation is likely.
- Type M / high-pressure threaded connections: 3.5–5 full threads engaged, cone/seat clean, weep holes clear.

---

## F.8 Quick Decision Flow

1. Is the hose within its maximum life? → No = Reject
2. Does it pass visual reject criteria? → No = Reject
3. Is annual pressure test current and passed? → No = Reject
4. Are whip checks present and correct on both sides? → No = Do not use until fitted
5. Are couplings correct for the application and undamaged? → No = Reject or re-terminate

**If in doubt → Quarantine and replace. Do not take a chance with high-pressure hose.**
