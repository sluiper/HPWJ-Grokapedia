# Appendix J: UHP / Creeping Hose Specific Card

**Status:** Draft on `draft/appendices`  
**Source:** MCR-034 + Section 7.17 + MCR-019/020/031/039  
**Purpose:** One-page field card for every UHP creeping-hose or 40 kpsi tube-bundle job. Highest-energy manual / semi-manual work Anabeeb performs.

---

## J.1 Absolute Requirements for UHP Creeping Hose (MCR-034)

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Level 3 operators only | ☐ | Method-specific competence + experience |
| AWD (Anti-Withdrawal Device) mandatory and verified on every lance | ☐ | MCR-019 / MCR-020 – life-critical |
| Vacuum at outlet (or equivalent capture) | ☐ | Controls debris and reaction |
| Expanded exclusion zone (typically 15–25 m+) | ☐ | MCR-031 UHP band |
| No quick-connects in high-movement run | ☐ | MCR-007 |
| Reaction force inside all three additive controls | ☐ | MCR-016 – even more critical at UHP |
| Hierarchy of methods considered (prefer automated / Bundle Blaster / 3D tooling where reasonably practicable) | ☐ | MCR-039 |

---

## J.2 Pre-Start Extra Checks (UHP Specific)

- [ ] Hose is within 2-year flex-lance life limit (MCR-001)
- [ ] Stinger / rigid leader length > pipe ID (MCR-022)
- [ ] Lance tip marked no closer than 600 mm from nozzle (MCR-052)
- [ ] Dump system tested and dedicated dump person identified if line-of-sight will be lost
- [ ] Medical Alert Cards carried + trauma kit immediately available
- [ ] Team briefed on back-out / ejection scenario (Case 2 fatality pattern)

---

## J.3 During Operation

- Continuous communication and line-of-sight (or dedicated dump person).
- Immediate dump if any loss of control, unusual reaction, or hose behaviour.
- No one in the line of fire or potential whip path.
- Stop and re-assess if the lance starts to bind or feed becomes uncontrolled.

---

## J.4 Hard Decision Gate

Is every item in J.1 a clear **YES**?

- **Yes** → Proceed under full UHP controls.
- **Any No** → **Do not start.** Switch to automated method or elevate to QHSSE / Project Manager for formal dispensation (rare and requires written justification).
