# Appendix H: Emergency Response & Medical Pocket Card

**Status:** Live on main (merged after clean verification)  
**Source:** Chapter 10 + MCR-043 + MCR-058 + Section 24 + DMAC  
**Purpose:** One-page card carried with the Medical Alert Card. Know exactly what to do if a high-pressure water injection injury occurs.

---

## HIGH-PRESSURE WATER INJECTION INJURY – IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

### 1. STOP THE PUMP / DUMP PRESSURE IMMEDIATELY
- Dump system or emergency stop.
- Do not delay for any other reason.

### 2. TREAT AS A MEDICAL EMERGENCY EVEN IF THE WOUND LOOKS SMALL
- High-pressure water (and entrained debris, chemicals, bacteria) can travel far along tissue planes.
- External wound size is **not** a reliable indicator of internal damage.
- Pain may be delayed.

### 3. FIRST AID ON SITE
- Control external bleeding with direct pressure (avoid tourniquet unless life-threatening limb haemorrhage).
- Do **not** probe, inject, or attempt to clean deep into the wound on site.
- Keep the injured person warm, still, and calm.
- Note the exact pressure, fluid type (water / chemical / seawater), and estimated volume if known.
- Keep the Medical Alert Card with the patient.

### 4. EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY TO MEDICAL FACILITY THAT UNDERSTANDS HPWJ INJURIES
- Inform the receiving facility that this is a **high-pressure water injection injury**.
- Request surgical evaluation (often requires exploration under anaesthesia).
- Antibiotics and tetanus status are almost always required.
- Imaging (X-ray / CT) may be needed to assess deeper damage.

### 5. INFORMATION TO HAND OVER
- Patient’s Medical Alert Card (Anabeeb OPS0080804 or equivalent)
- Operating pressure and fluid used
- Time of injury
- Any chemical additives or contaminants
- Whether the injury was on land or underwater (diving context changes decompression considerations)

---

## MEDICAL ALERT CARD REMINDER (MCR-043 / MCR-058)

Every operator and diver engaged in HPWJ work **must** carry an immediately accessible waterproof Medical Alert Card that states:

> “This person may have a high-pressure water injection injury. Treat as a surgical emergency. Do not underestimate the wound.”

Divers additionally require awareness of DMAC recommendations for high-pressure water jet injuries (MCR-058).
