# Chapter 15: HSE Assurance, Audit & Continuous Improvement

**Version 8.0**

## 15.1 Purpose

This chapter defines how Anabeeb assures that the controls in this Encyclopedia are actually implemented, how internal and client audits should be conducted against it, and how lessons are fed back into the Master Control Register, FMEA, and training. It cross-references the Regulatory & Client Matrix rather than duplicating its content.

## 15.2 Assurance Framework

Assurance rests on four layers already established in the repository:

1. **Master Control Register** – single source of truth for every rule and threshold.
2. **Field documents** – Pre-Use Inspection Checklist, Medical Alert Card, pocket cards, signed JSAs and permits (Chapters 5–6, 9–11, templates).
3. **Competency system** – Chapter 13 and MCR-030 / MCR-034 / MCR-036.
4. **Learning system** – Incident Case Study Library (Section 25) + FMEA (Section 20) + 30-day update rule after significant events.

## 15.3 Recommended Internal Audit Cadence

| Activity | Suggested Cadence | Focus |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Supervisor spot-check of pre-use inspection quality | Every job / shift (MCR-024) | Completeness and honesty of checklist |
| Field observation of high-RPN controls (AWD, reaction force, barricading, SIMOPS) | Weekly during active campaigns | MCR-016, 019/020, 027/031, 028 |
| Hose life and test-record audit | Monthly or per turnaround | MCR-001, MCR-002 |
| Competency register review | Quarterly | Expiry, Level vs task match (Ch13, MCR-030/034) |
| Full MCR / FMEA / Incident Library review | After any significant incident or near-miss (within 30 days) and at least annually | Living-document integrity |
| ATC training material vs MCR consistency check | Before each major course delivery | Chapter 13 + Section 25 |

These cadences are internal management tools. They do not create new technical thresholds.

## 15.4 What a Client (Aramco-style) Audit Would Typically Check

Cross-reference: `references/standards/21_Regulatory_and_Client_Matrix.md` and the expanded KSA / Anabeeb notes.

Typical lines of enquiry an Aramco / SABIC / PETRO RABIGH-style audit would follow against this Encyclopedia:

- Is there a documented, controlled set of rules (Master Control Register) that the workforce actually uses?
- Are pre-use inspections completed and signed for every job (MCR-023 / MCR-024 + template)?
- Are hoses within life and carrying current third-party test records (MCR-001 / MCR-002)?
- Is AWD used and verified for all flex-lance / creeping-hose work (MCR-019 / 020 / 034)?
- Are exclusion zones and barricading established before pressurising (MCR-027 / 031)?
- Is SIMOPS coordination evidenced when interfaces exist (MCR-028)?
- Are operators competent for the task and carrying Medical Alert Cards (Ch13, Ch10)?
- Is there a process for learning from incidents and updating controls (Section 25 + 30-day rule)?
- Can the contractor demonstrate CSMS-style HSE management (policy, risk assessment, operational control, emergency preparedness, continual improvement)?

The Encyclopedia is designed so that a positive answer to these questions can be evidenced from the MCR, completed checklists, competency register, and Incident Library.

## 15.5 Continuous Improvement Loop

1. Event occurs (incident, near-miss, audit finding, client observation, equipment failure).
2. Record in Incident Library (Section 25) within 30 days (anonymised as required).
3. Re-evaluate the relevant FMEA row(s) in Section 20.
4. Strengthen or add the corresponding MCR control if needed.
5. Update training material and field checklists.
6. Verify implementation on the next jobs (Chapter 12 supervisor accountability + MCR-036).

This loop keeps the system honest and prevents the Encyclopedia from becoming a static document.

## 15.6 Roles

- **Supervisor / Team Leader** – day-to-day assurance and stop-work authority (Chapter 12, MCR-036).
- **QHSSE / ATC Lead** – ownership of the MCR, FMEA, Incident Library, competency register, and audit programme.
- **All personnel** – stop-work authority and duty to report defects and near-misses.
