# Chapter 14: Maintenance, Reliability & Predictive Techniques

**Version 8.0**

## 14.1 Purpose

This chapter consolidates maintenance and reliability requirements already established in Chapter 7, Section 18, and the Master Control Register. It does **not** introduce new maintenance intervals or wear-life figures. All numeric limits are taken from existing MCR items and Chapter 7 content.

## 14.2 Existing Mandatory Intervals and Limits (No New Values)

| Item | Existing Rule | MCR / Source |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Flex lance / shotgun whip hose life | Maximum 2 years from manufacture or receipt | MCR-001 |
| All other high-pressure hoses | Maximum 4 years from manufacture or receipt | MCR-001 |
| Hose pressure testing | Annual third-party proof testing mandatory | MCR-002 |
| Nozzle wear life | Typically 20–60 hours (replace on wear or pitting) | MCR-018 / Ch7 |
| Rupture disc | Single-use only – replace after any activation; spare on unit | MCR-009 |
| Pre-use inspection | Full documented inspection before every use | MCR-023 |
| Inlet filtration | Multi-stage; monitor differential pressure | MCR-013 |
| NPSH margin | Maintain ≥ 0.5–1.0 bar above NPSHr | MCR-012 |

These are the enforceable intervals. No additional calendar or hour-based intervals are asserted in this chapter.

## 14.3 Preventive vs Predictive Maintenance

**Preventive (time- or use-based)**  
- Hose retirement at 2/4 years regardless of appearance (MCR-001).  
- Annual third-party hose pressure test (MCR-002).  
- Nozzle replacement within the 20–60 hour guidance or earlier if wear/pitting appears (MCR-018).  
- Rupture disc replacement after activation (MCR-009).  
- Regular fluid-end and power-end checks as described in Chapter 7.

**Predictive / condition-based (supporting, not replacing the above)**  
- Vibration and temperature trending on power end.  
- Filter differential-pressure monitoring (MCR-013).  
- Performance trending (pressure/flow decay may indicate wear or cavitation).  
- Visual and pin-gauge checks of nozzles and fittings at every pre-use inspection.

Predictive techniques improve reliability but do not override the hard life and testing rules already in the MCR.

## 14.4 Critical Spares and Turnaround Readiness

From Chapter 7 practice:
- Maintain spare rupture discs on every unit.
- Stage high-wear items (plungers, packing, valves, nozzles, whip checks) for major turnarounds.
- Keep hose stock within life limits and with current test records.

## 14.5 Post-Job and Storage Link

Maintenance effectiveness depends on correct post-job care and storage (Chapter 11 / MCR-035): depressurise, inspect, clean, store hoses properly (natural radius, protected from UV/heat), and complete records before leaving site.

## 14.6 Reliability Feedback Loop

- Any hose failure, rupture-disc activation, cavitation event, or unexpected wear must be recorded.
- Feed significant events into the Incident Library (Section 25) and re-evaluate the relevant FMEA row (Section 20).
- Update the Master Control Register if a new control or tighter limit is required.
