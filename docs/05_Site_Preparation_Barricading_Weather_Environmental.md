# Chapter 5: Site Preparation, Barricading, Weather & Environmental Controls

**Maximum Practical Depth – 10/10 Field Standard**  
**Version 7.6**

This chapter defines the mandatory requirements for preparing a safe HPWJ worksite, establishing exclusion zones, managing weather risks (especially critical in Jubail / KSA), and controlling environmental impacts. All content is linked to the Master Control Register and supports Chapters 6, 7, 10, and 13.

**Core Principle**: No HPWJ work starts until the site is properly prepared, barricaded, and weather conditions are confirmed acceptable.

---

## 5.1 Site Preparation Requirements

### Before Equipment Arrives
- Confirm work scope, pressure rating, and tooling with client/supervisor
- Identify all SIMOPS interfaces (hot work, lifting, scaffolding, vacuum trucks, confined spaces)
- Verify access routes for pump unit, hoses, vacuum truck, and emergency vehicles
- Confirm water supply quality and volume (clean, cool preferred for NPSH)
- Identify drainage, containment, and waste water disposal method
- Check for overhead power lines, buried services, and fragile structures
- Ensure adequate lighting for night or low-light work
- Confirm first-aid / trauma kit and Medical Alert Card availability

### On Arrival
- Position pump unit on stable, level ground with wheels chocked
- Orient exhaust and noise away from work areas and accommodation where possible
- Establish spill containment / bunding if required
- Stage hoses, tools, and spare rupture discs neatly and accessibly
- Verify grounding of the unit

## 5.2 Barricading & Exclusion Zones (MCR-027)

**Mandatory for all HPWJ operations.**

### Recommended Minimum Exclusion Zones (Industry Best Practice Synthesis)
| Operating Pressure | Minimum Exclusion Zone Radius | Notes |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|-------|
| Up to 10,000 psi (≈690 bar) | 7.5–10 m (25–30 ft) | Base distance |
| 10,000–20,000 psi | 10–15 m (30–50 ft) | Increase for open areas |
| 20,000–40,000+ psi (UHP / Creeping Hose) | 15–25 m+ (50–80 ft) | Significantly larger; adjust for jet direction and possible hose whip |

**Additional Rules**:
- Barricades must be physical (hard barriers, fencing, or robust tape with warning signs) and continuous.
- Signs must be bilingual (English + Arabic) where possible and clearly state “High Pressure Water Jetting – Authorised Personnel Only – Injection Injury Hazard”.
- Entry into the exclusion zone is strictly controlled. Only the HPWJ team and essential support (e.g. vacuum operator under control) may enter.
- Any unauthorised person entering the zone → immediate dump of pressure and stop work.
- For UHP creeping hose and tube bundle work, expand the zone in the direction of possible hose whip or back-spray.
- Screens or blast shields should be used where the jet could be directed toward other workers or sensitive equipment.

**WJTA / Safe Work Australia Alignment**: Barricading and restricted access is mandatory. People other than the operating team must be kept out of barricaded work areas.

## 5.3 Weather & Environmental Controls (Critical for KSA)

### Heat Stress (Highest Risk in Jubail Summer)
- Monitor WBGT or ambient temperature + humidity.
- Mandatory rest / shade / hydration schedule when temperatures exceed threshold (company heat stress procedure).
- Provide cool water, electrolyte replacement, and shaded rest areas.
- Adjust work/rest ratios; consider early morning / night shifts for extreme heat.
- Watch for heat exhaustion and heat stroke signs in all team members (especially those in full PPE).
- Link to Medical Alert Card and Chapter 10 response.

### Rain, Wind & Lightning
- **Lightning**: Stop all outdoor HPWJ work if thunder is heard or lightning is observed within range. Seek substantial shelter. Wait 30 minutes after last thunder before resuming (standard 30-30 rule).
- **High Wind**: Stop work if wind makes hose control, barricading, or stance unsafe, or if debris becomes a hazard. Secure all loose equipment.
- **Heavy Rain / Flooding**: Stop work if footing becomes slippery, visibility is reduced, or electrical hazards increase. Protect electrical components of the pump unit.
- **Dust / Shamal**: Protect inlet filtration; increase filter checks; consider respiratory protection if dust is severe.

### Other Environmental Controls
- Contain and dispose of waste water and debris according to client and environmental regulations.
- Prevent contamination of process equipment, soil, or drains.
- Noise: Position unit to minimise impact on nearby workers and residential areas; use hearing protection as required.
- Spill response materials must be immediately available.

---

## 5.4 Lighting, Access & Housekeeping

- Adequate lighting for the entire exclusion zone and access routes (especially night work).
- Clear, unobstructed escape routes from the work area.
- Hoses routed to avoid trip hazards and vehicle traffic where possible; use ramps or protection if crossing roads.
- Good housekeeping – no loose tools, unused hoses, or debris inside the exclusion zone.

---

## 5.5 Pre-Start Site Verification Checklist (Summary)

- [ ] Exclusion zone established and signed (correct size for pressure)
- [ ] Physical barricades in place and continuous
- [ ] SIMOPS interfaces controlled (Chapter 6)
- [ ] Weather conditions acceptable (heat, wind, lightning, rain)
- [ ] Heat stress controls in place if required
- [ ] Access and escape routes clear
- [ ] Lighting adequate
- [ ] Spill containment and waste plan confirmed
- [ ] First-aid / trauma kit and Medical Alert Cards present
- [ ] Pre-use inspection completed (templates/checklists)

---

## 5.6 Never Rules – Site Preparation

- Never start HPWJ without physical barricading and clear exclusion zone.
- Never allow unauthorised persons inside the barricaded area.
- Never continue work during lightning or unsafe weather.
- Never ignore heat stress controls in high temperatures.
- Never leave the site unsecured with pressurised equipment.
