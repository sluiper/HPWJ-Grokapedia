# Chapter 4: Planning, Technical Specification & Version Control

**Version 8.0**

## 4.1 Purpose

This chapter defines what must be done **before** a job reaches the field execution stage covered in Chapter 6. It sets out the planning inputs, the Master Control Register items that must be checked at the planning stage, technical specification requirements, and the version-control rules that keep procedures and this Encyclopedia consistent.  
Where content overlaps with Chapter 6 (Work Process, JSA, Permits, LOTO, SIMOPS), this chapter cross-references rather than duplicates.

## 4.2 Planning Stage – What Must Be Confirmed Before Mobilisation

Before equipment is mobilised or a permit is requested, the following must be established:

1. **Job scope and location** – exact equipment to be cleaned, access constraints, client permit system.
2. **Pressure and method class** – using the bands already defined in Chapter 3 (and MCR-031 exclusion-zone tiers).
3. **Tooling concept** – handheld, flex lance + AWD, creeping hose / UHP, automated, or combination (links to MCR-016, MCR-019/020, MCR-034).
4. **Team competency** – required Level(s) per Chapter 13 and MCR-030 / MCR-034.
5. **SIMOPS interfaces** – other work parties, hot work, lifting, vacuum trucks (MCR-028).
6. **Site conditions** – access, openings/pits (Chapter 5 + new candidate FMEA row), heat, lighting, water supply quality.
7. **Emergency response** – Medical Alert Cards, trauma kit, communication with client medical services (Chapter 10).

These inputs feed the Job Safety Analysis / Task Risk Assessment and the Permit-to-Work application described in Chapter 6.

## 4.3 MCR Items That Must Be Checked at Planning Stage

The following existing Master Control Register items are planning-stage gates (not only field gates):

| MCR ID | Planning Check |
|--------|----------------|
| MCR-016 | Reaction force will be manageable with the planned tooling and operators (three additive controls). |
| MCR-019 / 020 | If flex lance or creeping hose is planned, AWD method and verification are defined. |
| MCR-023 | Time and resources for full pre-use inspection are allocated (no production-pressure shortcut). |
| MCR-027 / 031 | Exclusion zone size and barricading method are defined for the planned pressure band. |
| MCR-028 | SIMOPS interfaces identified and coordination method agreed. |
| MCR-030 / 034 | Competency levels of named operators match the planned method (especially UHP). |
| MCR-033 | Heat-stress controls planned if ambient conditions require them. |
| MCR-009 / 010 | Rupture disc rating and dump-system arrangement confirmed for the planned pressure. |

If any of these cannot be satisfied, the method or resources must be changed before mobilisation.

## 4.4 Technical Specification Content (Minimum)

A job-specific technical specification or work pack should contain at least:
- Scope and equipment identification
- Planned pressure / flow / tooling
- Named competent operators and supervisor
- Reference to applicable MCR items and the Pre-Use Inspection Checklist
- Barricading and exclusion-zone plan
- SIMOPS and isolation interfaces
- Emergency response arrangements (including Medical Alert Card confirmation)
- Any client-specific permit or procedure numbers

Detailed execution steps, LOTO sequence, and permit flow remain in Chapter 6.

## 4.5 Version Control Rules for Procedures and this Encyclopedia

1. The Master Control Register is the single source of truth for every rule and numeric threshold. No procedure may introduce a conflicting threshold.
2. Any change that affects a rule, limit, or mandatory control must update the MCR first (or be flagged as a candidate MCR row).
3. Chapters, checklists, and training materials carry a version number and date.
4. Obsolete versions of controlled documents must be withdrawn from field use.
5. After any significant incident or near-miss, the Incident Library (Section 25), FMEA (Section 20), and MCR must be reviewed within 30 days.
6. Self-grading language is prohibited (see Chapter 1.5).

These rules keep the Encyclopedia and all field documents internally consistent.

## 4.6 Relationship to Chapter 6

- **This chapter (4)** covers pre-mobilisation planning, technical specification content, and version control.
- **Chapter 6** covers the operational work process once planning is complete: JSA finalisation, permit application and acceptance, isolation/LOTO, pre-job briefing, safe start-up, execution controls, and controlled shutdown hand-over to Chapter 11.

Do not duplicate the full JSA or permit-flow text here; refer to Chapter 6 for execution-stage detail.

## 4.7 Summary

Planning is complete only when the method, competency, exclusion zone, SIMOPS interfaces, reaction-force feasibility, and emergency arrangements have been checked against the relevant MCR items and a technical specification exists that the field team can execute under Chapter 6 controls.
