**Version 8.0**

## 2.1 Purpose of this Chapter

This chapter is a short fundamentals primer for operators, supervisors, and new personnel. It does **not** introduce new formulas, thresholds, or technical claims. All technical content is drawn from the already-verified material in Section 16 (Physics & Hydraulics) and Chapter 7 (Equipment). Its job is orientation, not original technical derivation.

## 2.2 What High Pressure Water Jetting Is

High Pressure Water Jetting (HPWJ) uses a pump to force water through a small orifice (nozzle) at high pressure. The pump displaces a volume of water; pressure is created by the restriction at the nozzle. The resulting high-velocity jet cleans, cuts, or removes material.

Key physical realities (already established in Section 16):
- Jet velocity rises with the square root of pressure (see Section 16.4 for verified values).
- The jet produces an equal and opposite reaction force on the tool and operator (Newton’s third law). This is why reaction-force limits exist (MCR-016).
- The same high velocity that makes the jet effective also makes it extremely dangerous to human tissue (high-pressure injection injury risk – Chapter 10).

## 2.3 Core System Components (from Chapter 7)

A typical HPWJ system consists of:
- **Power source** – diesel engine or electric motor.
- **High-pressure pump** – usually a triplex or quintuplex plunger pump (power end + fluid end).
- **High-pressure hoses and fittings** – life-managed, colour-coded, whip-checked (MCR-001 to MCR-008).
- **Pressure relief / dump system** – must be under the nozzle operator’s direct control (MCR-010).
- **Nozzle / tooling** – straight, fan, rotating, flex lance, automated heads, etc.
- **Safety devices** – rupture disc (MCR-009), Anti-Withdrawal Device for flex lances (MCR-019/020), whip checks (MCR-006).

Detailed selection, inspection, and failure modes are covered in Chapter 7 and Section 20.

## 2.4 The Most Important Safety Controls (Orientation Only)

These are not new rules; they are the highest-priority existing controls that every person on an HPWJ job must understand:

- **Pre-use inspection is mandatory** before every use (MCR-023). Highest RPN item.
- **Reaction force** must satisfy all three additive controls (MCR-016).
- **Flex lance work requires a correctly installed and verified AWD** every insertion (MCR-019 / MCR-020).
- **Never leave the pump running unattended** (MCR-011).
- **Rupture disc is single-use** – replace after any activation (MCR-009).
- **Barricading and exclusion zones** are mandatory (MCR-027 / MCR-031).
- **Medical Alert Card** must be carried; injection injuries are time-critical (Chapter 10).

Full detail and the complete list of controls live in the Master Control Register and the operational chapters.

## 2.5 How Pressure, Flow and Power Relate (Conceptual Only)

- Higher pressure generally increases cleaning power on hard deposits.
- Higher flow generally increases cleaning speed / coverage.
- Power requirement rises with the product of pressure and flow (see Section 16.10 and Chapter 7 for the established relationships). No new calculations are introduced here.

Plunger size changes on a given pump trade flow for pressure capability (and vice versa). After any plunger change, hose, fitting and rupture-disc ratings must be re-verified (MCR-015).
