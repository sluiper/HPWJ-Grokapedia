# Chapter 12: Supervisor & Team Leader Responsibilities

**Version 8.2 – Full Depth (SABIC OMS 8.2 + Anabeeb OPS-P-019 Dual Source)**  
**Primary Sources:** Anabeeb OPS-P-019 (company procedure), SABIC OMS Attachment 8.2 (mandatory KSA), YANSAB JSA practice, Master Control Register, Chapter 8, Chapter 13.

This chapter is the accountability backbone for Anabeeb HPWJ leadership and Level 4 ATC training. It defines non-negotiable duties for the Anabeeb Site Supervisor / HPWJ Supervisor, the Project Manager / Project in Charge, the HSE Supervisor, and (on SABIC sites) the client HPWJ Authorising Person. Quality is demonstrated by source mapping and Verification Log.

**Linked MCR:** MCR-023, MCR-024, MCR-030, MCR-036, MCR-038, MCR-039, MCR-041, MCR-042, MCR-047, MCR-049, Chapter 8, Chapter 11, Chapter 13.

## 12.1 Role Hierarchy & Non-Delegable Accountability

| Role | Source | Core Accountability |
|------|--------|---------------------|
| **Project Manager / Project in Charge** | OPS-P-019 §6.1 | Overall responsibility that the HPWJ decision tree / hierarchy of methods is followed and that Supervisors, Foremen and operators are aware of safety requirements, trained, certified and fit. **This responsibility cannot be delegated.** |
| **Site Supervisor – HPWJ (Anabeeb)** | OPS-P-019 §6.2; SABIC 2.7 | Basic equipment checks via visual pre-op checklist; hoses free of broken/rusted braid; O-rings/gaskets; filters; rupture disc ≤1.2× lowest MAWP; dump/trigger correct; nozzles not worn/plugged; fittings/lances/stingers rated ≥ pump MAWP. Primary liaison with client Permit Issuer. |
| **HSE Supervisor** | OPS-P-019 §6.3 | Immediate stop-work authority for any activity deemed dangerous or compromising safety of operators, standby personnel or persons in the vicinity. Practical advice. |
| **HPWJ Authorising Person (Client – SABIC)** | SABIC 2.2.1, 2.7.8 | Appointed by End-User Manager. Reviews Technical Specification (ATT-1), ensures GPS-approved contractor, leads JSA, supervises, periodic visits, verifies controls before permit. |
| **HP Jetter Operator** | OPS-P-019 §6.5 | Valid current certification for the process; hand signals; coordination; works to Safe System of Work. |
| **Emergency Button / Dump Operator** | OPS-P-019 §6.6 | Direct line of sight of both lance operator and pump operator; sole task is emergency reaction. |

**MCR-036 + MCR-041:** Supervisor and (on SABIC sites) Authorising Person are jointly accountable for competency verification, pre-use quality, SIMOPS and stop-work authority. Project Manager retains non-delegable overall responsibility under Anabeeb procedure.

## 12.2 Pre-Job Duties of the Anabeeb HPWJ Supervisor (Absolute)

Before any pressurisation the Supervisor shall personally ensure:

### 12.2.1 Competency Gate (MCR-030 / 042 / 049)
- Every team member holds current, method-specific certification (Anabeeb Training Dept or TQCP/equivalent).
- Manual operators have **minimum 2 years continuous** relevant experience recorded on training card (OPS-P-019).
- Management approval obtained for any manual lancing / gun operation.
- Automatic-machine qualification does **not** authorise manual work.
- Medical fitness confirmed; Medical Alert Card (OPS0080804 or equivalent) in possession of every crew member (MCR-043).
- Team size meets minimum: **3 competent persons** for Anabeeb operations (MCR-047); dedicated emergency-stop person when line of sight will be lost.

### 12.2.2 Documentation Gate
- Technical Specification (SABIC ATT-1 or Anabeeb equivalent) agreed and attached.
- Formal JSA / RA completed using hierarchy of methods (MCR-039) and Table 4.1 risk guidance.
- Work Permit request prepared; on SABIC sites ATT-6 Pre-Job Checklist is pre-requisite for HPWJ Work Permit.
- Hierarchy of methods decision recorded: automated preferred; manual requires elevated approval; flexible lance without AWD = Not Permitted.

### 12.2.3 Equipment & Site Gate (MCR-023 / 024)
- Full Pre-Use / daily Safety Checklist completed and signed (Anabeeb forms + SABIC ATT-6 items).
- Personally verify (or spot-check and confirm):
  - Rupture disc correctly rated (≤1.2× lowest MAWP) and spare on unit (MCR-048 / 009).
  - Dump / dead-man / foot valve tested and functional (MCR-010).
  - Hoses free of broken/rusted braid, kinks, cuts; annual test current; life not exceeded (MCR-001–004).
  - Whip-checks / anti-whip devices and safety shroud / Burst Protection Sleeve (≥6 ft) fitted (MCR-006 / 045).
  - AWD / hose catcher / stinger correctly installed for any flex/lancing work (MCR-019 / 020).
  - Lance length ≥1.2 m (or special RA for shorter + double trigger) (MCR-044).
  - Lance tip marked ≥600 mm from nozzle (MCR-052).
  - No quick-connects on high-pressure hoses (MCR-007).
  - System leak-tested at maximum anticipated pressure.
  - Pump grounded; hazardous-area classification respected.
  - Barricading / exclusion zone correct: SABIC min 6 m (or approved screens); Anabeeb unauthorised persons excluded to **10 m** (MCR-027 / 031 / 051).
  - Steel guarding / deflection boards / back-end tube shields in place where required.
  - Environmental controls (drainage, bunding, waste route) confirmed.
  - Sensitive / electrical equipment protected.

### 12.2.4 Briefing Gate (MCR-025)
- Lead pre-job / toolbox talk covering:
  - Hierarchy of methods decision and Never Rules (Chapter 9).
  - Exact pressure, flow, nozzle, reaction-force calculation (≤250 N).
  - Hand signals and emergency dump command.
  - Communication protocol (no blind spots).
  - Heat stress / work-rest / rotation plan (MCR-033).
  - Emergency response and Medical Alert Card wording (Chapter 10).
  - SIMOPS interfaces and stop-work authority.
- Every team member (including Supervisor) endorses the JSA / Technical Specification to demonstrate understanding.

Only after all four gates are closed does the Supervisor accept the HPWJ Work Permit and allow pressurisation.

## 12.3 During-Execution Duties

1. Maintain continuous overall situational awareness of the jetting area, exclusion zone, hose behaviour, and crew condition.
2. Enforce communication protocol; any loss of communication or line-of-sight without dedicated dump person = immediate dump and stop.
3. Intervene immediately on any breach of Never Rules, reaction-force limits, AWD status, or unauthorised entry (10 m Anabeeb / 6 m SABIC).
4. Manage fatigue and heat stress: enforce rotation, work/rest, hydration, shade; never allow a fatigued operator to continue controlling a high-energy lance.
5. Coordinate with other supervisors and client Authorising Person in SIMOPS environments (MCR-028).
6. Ensure only team members are inside the barrier while jetting; any inspection entry requires suspension of jetting and Supervisor escort.
7. On SABIC sites, support the Authorising Person’s periodic verification visits.
8. Stop work on any change or deviation; notify Permit Issuer and Authorising Person / Project in Charge; re-assess before restart.

---

## 12.4 Post-Job Duties (Link to Chapter 11)

1. Ensure controlled shutdown sequence is followed exactly (MCR-037 / Chapter 11.1).
2. Ensure full post-job inspection of hoses, fittings, AWD, nozzles, rupture disc status, and safety devices (MCR-035).
3. Ensure decontamination, correct coiling/storage, and tagging of any defective equipment.
4. Review any near-misses, deviations or lessons with the team; feed into MCR / FMEA / ATC training.
5. Complete and sign all records (Pre-Use Checklist, permit close-out, hours, condition notes, lessons).
6. Escalate any equipment, competency or control failures to QHSSE / Project Manager immediately.
7. Confirm site restored to client satisfaction (barriers, signs, debris removed).

---

## 12.5 Stop-Work Authority & Escalation

- **Any** team member can call stop-work. The call is absolute (MCR-038).
- Supervisor supports the call and never allows production pressure or schedule to override the Master Control Register or OPS-P-019 / SABIC controls.
- HSE Supervisor has independent stop-work authority for any dangerous condition.
- Changes or deviations → stop → depressurise → notify → re-assess / re-permit if required.
- Post-stop: full controlled shutdown (Chapter 11) before investigation or restart.

---

## 12.6 Never Rules Specific to Supervisors / Project Managers

- Never allow an untrained, expired, or under-experienced (manual <2 years) person to operate.
- Never sign off or accept an incomplete Pre-Use Checklist or ATT-6.
- Never ignore a stop-work call or a communication failure.
- Never prioritise schedule over MCR / OPS-P-019 / SABIC controls.
- Never permit flexible lance work without verified AWD / catcher.
- Never permit hand-held shotgunning above 10 000 psi with orifice >1.6 mm or reaction force >250 N.
- Never permit unauthorised persons inside the 10 m (Anabeeb) / 6 m (SABIC) zone while pressurised.
- Never leave the overall decision-tree / hierarchy-of-methods responsibility unfulfilled (Project Manager – non-delegable).
- Never leave site with incomplete records or a pressurised system.

## 12.7 Sample Pre-Job Supervisor Verification Script (Toolbox)

1. “Confirm every person has current method-specific card and Medical Alert Card – show me.”
2. “Manual operators: confirm 2-year continuous experience on card and Management approval for this manual job.”
3. “Team is three: lance, pump, emergency dump. Who is dump if LOS lost?”
4. “Hierarchy decision: automated / manual with strain relief / manual reaction-force? Approval level?”
5. “Reaction force calculated? ≤250 N? Show numbers.”
6. “AWD / catcher / stinger / tip mark 600 mm / shroud 6 ft / whip-checks – verified by me.”
7. “Barricade 10 m unauthorised / 6 m baseline; signs up; shields in place.”
8. “Hand signals and dump command – demonstrate.”
9. “Heat plan: rotation times, rest area, water. Any heat symptoms = stop.”
10. “Stop-work authority is absolute. Any breach or unauthorised entry = dump and stop. Questions?”

All affirm → sign JSA / checklist → accept permit → proceed.

## 12.8 Link to Competency Framework (Chapter 13) & ATC

- Level 4 (Supervisor / Team Leader) assessment must include practical demonstration of the four pre-job gates, the verification script, stop-work exercise, and post-job record close-out.
- Project Manager awareness module should cover the non-delegable accountability statement in OPS-P-019 §6.1.
- Observations of Supervisor performance feed back into competency register and MCR continuous improvement (Chapter 15).
