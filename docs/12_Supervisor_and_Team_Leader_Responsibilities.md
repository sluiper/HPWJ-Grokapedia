# Chapter 12: Supervisor & Team Leader Responsibilities

**Version 8.0**

The Supervisor / Team Leader is the critical link between the Master Control Register, the competency system, and real-world execution. This chapter defines non-negotiable accountabilities.

**Linked MCR:** MCR-024, MCR-036, MCR-023, MCR-028, MCR-030, Chapter 13 Level 4.

---

## 12.1 Core Accountabilities

The Supervisor is personally accountable for:
1. Verifying that every team member holds current, appropriate competency (Chapter 13).
2. Ensuring the Pre-Use Inspection is completed to standard and spot-checking quality (MCR-024).
3. Confirming physical barricading, correct exclusion zone, and SIMOPS controls are in place before pressurising.
4. Leading the pre-job briefing and ensuring all team members understand the Never Rules and emergency response.
5. Maintaining stop-work authority and supporting any team member who exercises it.
6. Ensuring Medical Alert Cards are carried and the trauma kit is available.
7. Controlling SIMOPS interfaces and stopping work if controls break down.
8. Completing accurate records and feeding lessons into the MCR and FMEA.
9. Never allowing production pressure to override safety controls.

---

## 12.2 Pre-Job Duties
- Review JSA / TRA and permit.
- Verify competency cards / register for all operators.
- Lead toolbox talk covering MCR high-priority items, weather, SIMOPS, and Chapter 10 response.
- Confirm Pre-Use Checklist is fully completed and signed.
- Personally verify AWD (if flex lance), whip checks, and rupture disc.
- Confirm exclusion zone size is appropriate for the planned pressure (especially UHP).

---

## 12.3 During Execution
- Maintain overall situational awareness.
- Enforce communication protocol.
- Intervene immediately on any breach of Never Rules or loss of control.
- Manage fatigue, heat stress, and crew rotation.
- Coordinate with other supervisors in SIMOPS environments.

---

## 12.4 Post-Job Duties
- Ensure controlled shutdown and post-job inspection are completed (Chapter 11).
- Review any near-misses or lessons with the team.
- Sign off records and escalate any issues to QHSSE.
- Update competency observations if required.

---

## 12.5 Never Rules for Supervisors
- Never allow an untrained or expired person to operate.
- Never sign off an incomplete Pre-Use Checklist.
- Never ignore a stop-work call.
- Never prioritise schedule over the Master Control Register.
