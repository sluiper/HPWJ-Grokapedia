# Chapter 11: Shutdown, Post-Job, Decontamination, Care, Storage & Records

**Version 8.2 – Full Depth (SABIC OMS 8.2 + Anabeeb OPS-P-019 Dual Source)**  
**Primary Sources:** Anabeeb OPS-P-019, SABIC OMS Attachment 8.2 (Do’s/Don’ts, work process, effluent), YANSAB environmental controls, Master Control Register, Chapter 7, Chapter 18, Section 28.

This chapter closes the operational loop. A perfect start means nothing if the end of the job is poorly controlled. Proper shutdown, care, decontamination and records protect people, equipment life, the environment, client relations, and Anabeeb’s reputation and CSMS/audit standing.

**Linked MCR:** MCR-001 to MCR-006, MCR-009, MCR-011, MCR-023, MCR-035, MCR-037, MCR-038, MCR-048, Chapter 8, Chapter 12, Section 28.

## 11.1 Controlled Shutdown Sequence (Mandatory – MCR-037)

The sequence is absolute. Never reverse the order. Never leave a pressurised or running system.

1. **Lance / gun operator** reduces pressure in a controlled manner (never slam valves – water-hammer risk, MCR-029).
2. Confirm system is fully depressurised: gauges read zero, dump / dead-man open and verified, no residual pressure in hoses.
3. **Pump operator** shuts down the unit according to manufacturer procedure (engine cool-down if diesel, electrical isolation if electric).
4. Apply LOTO / isolation if the unit will be left unattended or if any maintenance, nozzle change, or hose work will occur (MCR-026).
5. Secure all hoses and tools to prevent trip / whip hazards; fit protective caps on open ends where appropriate.
6. Only then begin decontamination, inspection and packing.

**SABIC / Anabeeb alignment:** Both require full pressure release on any shutdown and before any adjustment or repair. OPS-P-019 and SABIC Do’s explicitly forbid tightening any pressure joint under pressure and require rectification of all leaks only after depressurisation.

**Never leave equipment running or pressurised unattended (MCR-011).**

## 11.2 Decontamination & Environmental Close-Out (SABIC 2.4.2 / 2.5 + OPS-P-019 §10 + Section 28)

### 11.2.1 Process Residue & Wash Water
- Flush hoses, lances, guns and tools with clean water if process contaminants, scale, coatings or chemicals were present.
- Collect and dispose of process residue, scale, chemical residue and contaminated wash water according to the **client environmental controls** agreed at planning stage (Technical Specification / ATT-1 / JSA).
- On SABIC / YANSAB sites: residue routed to designated drain system; continuous level monitoring; stop if drain reaches ~90 % or blockage occurs; mesh/strainer to prevent large debris entering drains; drip tray under machine for oil leakage.
- Special facilities may be required for toxic / hazardous debris – confirm with Client Environmental team before demobilisation.
- Never allow uncontrolled discharge to soil, storm drains or water courses.

### 11.2.2 Equipment Exterior
- Clean exterior of pump unit, cooling fins, filters, electrical enclosures and gauges.
- Protect against flash rusting of cleaned surfaces if required by client (inhibitor use – manufacturer / process guidance).
- Inspect for damage or contamination that requires quarantine or further cleaning before next mobilisation.

### 11.2.3 Waste Segregation
- Segregate clean water, oily water, chemical-laden water and solid debris as required by site permit / Royal Commission / client rules.
- Complete any waste transfer notes or environmental close-out forms required by the Work Permit.

## 11.3 Post-Job Equipment Inspection & Care (MCR-035)

Perform immediately after depressurisation and before transport / storage. Record findings.

### 11.3.1 Hoses & Connections (MCR-001 to MCR-006)
- Full visual inspection against reject criteria (MCR-004): exposed braid, bulging, cuts to reinforcement, kinks, weepage, damaged fittings, exceeded life, missing marking.
- Check whip-checks / anti-whip devices and safety shrouds / Burst Protection Sleeves for damage or permanent deformation.
- Confirm couplings, Type-M seats, weep holes clean and undamaged.
- Coil correctly (natural radius, no forced tight bends, no kinks).
- Tag any hose requiring further testing, repair or retirement; quarantine tagged items.
- Update hose life register, last-test date and hours if tracked.
- Flex lances / shotgun whips: max 2 years; all other HP hoses: max 4 years (MCR-001).

### 11.3.2 Pump, Fluid End & Safety Devices
- Check for leaks, unusual wear, vibration marks, gauge condition and accuracy.
- Confirm rupture disc status: if fired or damaged, replace with correctly rated disc (≤1.2× lowest MAWP – MCR-048) before next use; replenish spare on unit (MCR-009).
- Verify dump / dead-man / foot valve still free and functional.
- Top up fluids (power-end oil, coolant, fuel) if required; record.
- Clean and protect exposed plungers / packing per manufacturer recommendation.
- Note any cavitation signs (gravel noise history, pitting) for maintenance planning (Chapter 14 / MCR-012).

### 11.3.3 Nozzles, Lances, AWD & Tooling
- Clean orifices and threads; inspect for wear, pitting, out-of-round (MCR-018).
- Record hours used against 20–60 h typical wear life.
- Confirm AWD / hose catcher / stinger condition and function; store properly.
- Check lance barrel straightness, trigger condition, double-trigger function if fitted.
- Confirm tip-mark tape/paint still present and legible (≥600 mm – MCR-052).

### 11.3.4 Automated / Mechanised Tooling
- For 2XLTC, 3XLTC, IBC, OBC, Bundle Blaster, 3D heads, xPRO modules etc.: follow manufacturer post-use inspection and storage; test sensors / auto-shut-off before next mobilisation (MCR-021).

## 11.4 Storage Requirements

- **Hoses:** racks or reels, out of direct sunlight, off the ground, protected from UV, heat, chemicals, vehicles and sharp edges. Maintain natural coil radius. Clearly label pressure rating, expiration / manufacture date, last test date and next due.
- **Pump unit:** clean, dry, secure location; wheels chocked; electrical components and gauges protected; covers fitted; fuel/DEF secured if diesel.
- **Lances / guns / AWD / nozzles:** clean, dry, protected threads/orifices; organised so next Pre-Use is efficient.
- **PPE (suits, gaiters, gloves, visors):** cleaned per manufacturer (TST / Safetech / TurtleSkin instructions); inspected for cuts, abrasion or jet damage; hung or stored to dry; Air Comfort components cleaned and hoses drained if used.
- Never store unserviceable equipment without quarantine tagging and notification to Supervisor / Store (OPS-P-019 Don’ts).

## 11.5 Records (Mandatory – MCR-035 + Client / TVTC / CSMS)

Before leaving site, complete, sign and file (or upload to client system):

| Record | Purpose | Retention |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Pre-Use / Daily Safety Checklist (signed) | Proof of MCR-023 gate | Min 5 years or client/TVTC |
| Work Permit close-out / sign-off | Legal / client PTW system | Per client |
| Hose & equipment condition notes / tags | Life management, next Pre-Use | Life of asset + 5 years |
| Running hours / nozzle hours (if tracked) | Wear life, maintenance | Life of asset |
| Near-miss / incident / stop-work reports | Learning, MCR/FMEA update | Per QHSSE / GOSI |
| Lessons learned (even if “none”) | Continuous improvement (Ch15) | ATC / QHSSE |
| Environmental / waste transfer notes | Client & RC compliance | Per environmental rules |
| Competency observations (if any) | Chapter 13 register | Per competency system |
| Medical Alert Card issue log (if new cards issued) | Audit readiness | Per medical / ATC |

**Anabeeb forms** (controlled): OPS0020804 (flex lance/hose visual), OPS0050804 (hose visual testing), OPS0060804 (guns), OPS0070804 (foot controls), OPS0080804 (Medical Card), plus Pre-Job Checklist and permit forms.

**SABIC / client:** ATT-6 close-out, permit return, any Technical Specification close-out notes.

Incomplete records = incomplete job. Supervisor does not leave site until records are closed (Chapter 12.4).

## 11.6 Site Restoration

- Remove all barriers, warning signs, deflection boards and temporary shields only after final depressurisation and client agreement.
- Clear debris, spent nozzles, packaging and waste to client satisfaction.
- Restore any temporary access, scaffolding or platforms per original condition or client instruction.
- Confirm with Area Owner / Permit Issuer that the site is accepted before final sign-off.

---

## 11.7 Never Rules – End of Job (MCR-038)

- Never leave the site with a pressurised or running system.
- Never skip post-job inspection of hoses, rupture disc, dump system, AWD or critical safety devices.
- Never store hoses in direct sunlight or with forced tight bends / kinks.
- Never store unserviceable equipment without quarantine tag and notification.
- Never leave incomplete records or unsigned checklists / permits.
- Never allow uncontrolled discharge of contaminated wash water or debris.
- Never remove barricades while any residual pressure or unauthorised risk remains.
- Never leave without notifying the Work Permit Issuer / Area Owner and obtaining close-out signature.

**Always (key Do’s from SABIC / OPS-P-019):**
- Release all pressure on any shutdown.
- Strip, clean and store equipment in clean condition.
- Clear site of barriers, signs and debris to customer’s satisfaction.
- Ensure all paperwork (satisfaction notes, work sheets, permits, checklists) is completed and signed.
- Feed any lessons into the Master Control Register and ATC training loop.

## 11.8 Link to Continuous Improvement (Chapter 15) & FMEA

Post-job is the natural capture point for:
- Equipment wear or near-failure data → Chapter 14 / manufacturer feedback.
- Control effectiveness observations → FMEA RPN review (Section 20).
- Human-factor / fatigue / heat issues → Section 26 / MCR-033.
- Client or Authorising Person feedback → KSA notes / compliance matrix.
- Any new Never Rule or threshold → immediate MCR addition.

Supervisor and Project Manager are accountable for ensuring this feedback loop is not skipped (MCR-036).
