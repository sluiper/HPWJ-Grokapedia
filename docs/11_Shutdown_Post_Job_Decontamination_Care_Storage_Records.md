# Chapter 11: Shutdown, Post-Job, Decontamination, Care, Storage & Records

**Maximum Practical Depth – Completing the Job Lifecycle**  
**Version 7.6 – 10/10**

This chapter closes the operational loop. A perfect start means nothing if the end of the job is poorly controlled. Proper shutdown, care, and records protect people, equipment life, the environment, and Anabeeb’s reputation.

**Linked MCR Items:** MCR-035, MCR-037, MCR-009, MCR-011, MCR-001 to MCR-006, MCR-023.

---

## 11.1 Controlled Shutdown Sequence (Mandatory)

1. Nozzle operator reduces pressure in a controlled manner (never slam).
2. Confirm system is fully depressurised (gauges zero, dump open).
3. Pump operator shuts down the unit according to manufacturer procedure.
4. Apply LOTO if the unit will be left unattended or if any maintenance will occur.
5. Secure all hoses and tools to prevent trip/whip hazards.
6. Only then begin decontamination and packing.

**Never leave a pressurised or running system.**

---

## 11.2 Decontamination

- Flush hoses and tools with clean water if process contaminants were present.
- Remove and properly dispose of any process residue, scale, or chemical residue according to client environmental rules.
- Clean exterior of pump unit, especially cooling fins, filters, and electrical components.
- Inspect for any damage or contamination that requires quarantine.

---

## 11.3 Post-Job Equipment Inspection & Care

### Hoses
- Full visual inspection using the reject criteria (MCR-004).
- Check whip checks condition.
- Coil correctly (natural radius, no kinks).
- Tag any hose that requires further testing or retirement.
- Update hose life and test records.

### Pump & Tools
- Check for leaks, unusual wear, gauge condition, rupture disc status.
- Top up fluids if required.
- Clean and protect exposed plungers/packing if recommended by manufacturer.
- Verify spare rupture disc stock is replenished.

### Nozzles & AWD
- Clean, inspect orifices and threads.
- Record hours used against wear life.
- Store AWD and safety devices properly.

---

## 11.4 Storage Requirements

- Store hoses on proper racks or reels, out of direct sunlight, off the ground, protected from UV, heat, chemicals, and vehicles.
- Maintain natural coil radius.
- Clearly label with pressure rating, expiration, and last test date.
- Store pump unit in a clean, dry, secure location with wheels chocked.
- Protect electrical components and gauges.

---

## 11.5 Records (Mandatory)

Before leaving site, complete and file:
- Pre-Use Inspection Checklist (signed)
- Any near-miss or incident report
- Hose and equipment condition notes
- Permit close-out
- Running hours / nozzle hours if tracked
- Lessons learned (even if “none”) for continuous improvement

Records retained minimum 5 years or per client/TVTC requirement.

---

## 11.6 Never Rules – End of Job

- Never leave the site with a pressurised system.
- Never skip post-job inspection of hoses and critical safety devices.
- Never store hoses in direct sunlight or with forced tight bends.
- Never leave incomplete records.
