# Chapter 1: Introduction, Scope, Purpose & How to Use

**Version 8.0**

## 1.1 Purpose of this Encyclopedia

This Encyclopedia (HPWJ-Grokapedia) is Anabeeb’s single, controlled reference for High Pressure Water Jetting (HPWJ / Hydroblasting) operations. It exists to:

- Provide one authoritative source of truth for rules, thresholds, and procedures.
- Support safe field execution, competency development at the Anabeeb Training Center (ATC), and client audits.
- Link every operational control to the Master Control Register (MCR) so that training, checklists, and procedures remain consistent.
- Capture lessons from real incidents (documented and composite) and feed them back into the FMEA and MCR.

It is written for Anabeeb supervisors, operators, QHSSE personnel, ATC trainers, and client-facing technical staff working in the Eastern Province of Saudi Arabia and similar industrial environments.

## 1.2 Scope

**In scope:**
- High-pressure and ultra-high-pressure water jetting for industrial cleaning (vessels, heat exchangers, tube bundles, piping, surface preparation).
- Equipment selection, pre-use inspection, hose management, reaction force control, AWD use, SIMOPS, LOTO, emergency response (including high-pressure injection injury), competency, and post-job care.
- Alignment with global good practice (WJTA, WJA, AS/NZS 4233) adapted for Anabeeb / KSA operating conditions.

**Out of scope (for now):**
- Concrete cutting / demolition with water jets.
- Abrasive water-jet cutting systems.
- Detailed mechanical design of pumps or nozzles (refer to manufacturer data).
- Full client-specific SAES / CSMS clause extraction (this remains a living gap flagged in the KSA notes).

## 1.3 Single Source of Truth – Master Control Register

The **Master Control Register** (`MASTER_CONTROL_REGISTER.md`) is the single source of truth for every named rule, numeric threshold, and mandatory control.  
Every chapter, checklist, and training module must map its requirements back to MCR item IDs.  
If a new threshold is needed, it must be added to the MCR first (or flagged as a candidate) rather than asserted only in a chapter.

## 1.4 How to Use This Encyclopedia

1. **Field / Pre-job** – Use the Pre-Use Inspection Checklist, Reaction Force Quick Reference, Whip Check / Hose Card, Medical Alert Card, and the Never Rules (Chapter 9). Cross-check high-priority MCR items.
2. **Planning & Permit** – Chapters 5, 6, and 8 plus the relevant MCR rows.
3. **Training (ATC)** – Chapter 13 (Competency Framework) + Incident Case Study Library (Section 25) + the top RPN failure modes from Section 20.
4. **Incident / Near-miss** – Update Section 25, re-evaluate the FMEA, and add or strengthen the corresponding MCR row within 30 days.
5. **Audit / Client interface** – Master Control Register + Regulatory & Client Matrix + Chapter 12 (Supervisor Accountability).

## 1.5 Version Control & Living Document Rules

- All changes that affect a rule or threshold must update the Master Control Register.
- Chapters carry a version number and date.
- Self-grading language (“world best”, “10/10”, “no material errors”) is prohibited. Quality is demonstrated by the Verification Log and citations, not by assertion.
- After any real Anabeeb incident or significant near-miss, the Incident Library, FMEA, and MCR must be reviewed.

## 1.6 Structure Overview

- **Part 1 – Operational Core**: Chapters 5–13 (Site, Work Process, Equipment, Operational Control, Rules, Trauma, Shutdown, Supervisor, Competency).
- **Part 2 – Technical Reference**: Sections 16 (Physics), 18 (Hose), 20 (FMEA), 25 (Incident Library).
- **Supporting**: Templates, Master Control Register, Standards library, Manufacturer summaries.

Chapters 1–4 provide the entry point and classification framework so that someone opening the repository cold can orient themselves before diving into the operational chapters.
