# Section 28: Environmental Controls & Sustainability

**Version 8.0**

## 28.1 Purpose

This section consolidates environmental considerations already present or implied in the Encyclopedia (primarily Chapter 5 site preparation, manufacturer notes on water systems, and the Regulatory & Client Matrix). It does **not** introduce new numeric discharge limits, noise limits, or regulatory thresholds unless they already appear in the KSA / Regulatory Matrix files or other existing repository content. Any such figure that is not already recorded is flagged **[UNVERIFIED]**.

## 28.2 Water Usage and Discharge

High-pressure water jetting is water-intensive. Environmental control focuses on:
- Source water quality and volume planning (already required for NPSH and pump protection – MCR-012 / 013).
- Containment and controlled disposal of runoff, especially when the jet has contacted process residues, scale, coatings, hydrocarbons, or other contaminants.
- Prevention of uncontrolled discharge to soil, drains, or water courses.

**Already established practice (Chapter 5):**
- Identify drainage, containment, and waste-water disposal method before equipment arrives.
- Establish spill containment / bunding if required.
- Contain and dispose of waste water and debris according to client and environmental regulations.

No new volume-per-hour or discharge-concentration limits are asserted here. Client and regulatory limits (Aramco, SABIC, RC, Civil Defense, or local environmental authority) take precedence and must be confirmed for each site. Where specific numeric limits are not already captured in the Regulatory & Client Matrix, they remain **[UNVERIFIED — confirm against current client / RC / environmental permit]**.

## 28.3 Contaminated Runoff and Containment

When jetting removes process deposits, coatings, or hydrocarbons, the runoff becomes a waste stream that may be hazardous. Controls already implied or stated:
- Physical containment (bunds, temporary dikes, vacuum recovery).
- Vacuum truck integration (especially for tube-bundle and vessel work – already emphasised for UHP creeping-hose operations in Chapter 7.17 and MCR-034).
- Segregation of clean vs contaminated water streams where required by the client or permit.
- Prevention of flash-to-drain of oily or chemical-laden water.

**Observation (not a new rule):** Several manufacturer summaries note recirculating or reclaim systems on certain equipment (e.g., some hydroblasting / surface-preparation systems). Where reclaim is available and permitted, it reduces fresh-water demand and discharge volume. Specific recovery rates or purity claims remain manufacturer-data dependent and are not generalised here.

## 28.4 Noise

HPWJ pumps (especially diesel units) and high-velocity jets generate significant noise. Chapter 5 already requires:
- Positioning the unit to minimise impact on nearby workers and accommodation where possible.
- Use of hearing protection as required.

No new dB(A) limits or distance-based noise contours are introduced. Client site rules and occupational noise exposure standards apply; exact numeric values not already in the repository are **[UNVERIFIED — refer to client site rules and applicable occupational exposure limits]**.

## 28.6 Sustainability Observations (Not New Rules)

- Water reclaim / recirculation systems (where fitted and permitted) reduce both consumption and discharge volume.
- Correct nozzle selection and standoff (Section 16 / 19) improve cleaning efficiency and can reduce total water and energy used per square metre cleaned.
- Predictive and preventive maintenance (Chapter 14) reduces unplanned failures that can lead to spills or inefficient high-water-use rework.
- These are observations drawn from existing content; they do not create new enforceable thresholds.

## 28.7 Planning and Audit Link

Environmental controls are planning-stage and audit-stage items (Chapters 4 and 15):
- Confirm disposal route and any permit requirements before mobilisation.
- Include containment and vacuum arrangements in the technical specification / work pack.
- Client (Aramco-style) audits typically check that waste water and contaminated debris are controlled and that spill response is available — see Chapter 15 and the Regulatory & Client Matrix.
