# Chapter 6: Work Process, JSA, Permit Flow, Isolation Verification & Pump LOTO, SIMOPS

**Maximum Practical Depth – Operational Backbone**  
**Version 7.6 – 10/10 Field & Client Ready**

This chapter is the operational heart of safe HPWJ work. It defines the complete work process from planning through execution to handover, with hard links to the Master Control Register, Chapter 10 (Trauma), Chapter 13 (Competency), and Chapter 7 (Equipment).

**Core Principle**: No HPWJ work starts without a valid permit, verified isolation (where required), competent team, completed pre-use inspection, and clear SIMOPS control.

---

## 6.1 Overall Work Process Flow (Anabeeb Standard)

1. Job request / scope definition
2. Technical planning & risk assessment (JSA / TRA)
3. Equipment selection & mobilisation
4. Permit-to-Work application & approval
5. Pre-job briefing & competency verification
6. Site preparation & barricading (link Ch5)
7. Pre-use inspection (mandatory checklist)
8. Isolation verification & Pump LOTO (if required)
9. Safe start-up & execution
10. Continuous monitoring & communication
11. Controlled shutdown & decontamination
12. Post-job inspection, records & handover
13. Lessons learned & MCR/FMEA feedback

---

## 6.2 Job Safety Analysis (JSA) / Task Risk Assessment

**Mandatory for every HPWJ job.**

### Minimum Content of HPWJ JSA
- Scope of work and exact location
- Pressure, flow, nozzle type, tooling (including UHP / creeping hose if used)
- Team composition and competency levels (must match Chapter 13)
- All high-priority MCR controls that apply
- Specific hazards: injection injury, reaction force, hose failure, SIMOPS interfaces, heat stress, confined space, hot work nearby, etc.
- Hierarchy of controls applied
- Emergency response plan (reference Chapter 10) and Medical Alert Card confirmation
- Barricading and exclusion zone plan
- Communication method
- Residual risk rating and acceptance

**JSA must be reviewed and signed by the Supervisor and all team members before work starts.**

## 6.3 Permit-to-Work Flow

### Standard HPWJ Permit Requirements
- Valid Permit-to-Work (or equivalent client system: Aramco, SABIC, PETRO RABIGH, etc.)
- Clear isolation certificate if energy isolation is required
- Confined space entry permit if applicable
- Hot work permit if SIMOPS with hot work exists
- Gas testing / atmospheric monitoring records if required

### Permit Content Must Include
- Exact work description and pressure rating
- Named competent Operator(s) and Supervisor
- Barricading requirements
- Emergency contacts and Medical Alert confirmation
- Validity period and shift handover rules

**Rule**: If the permit is incomplete or the isolations are not verified → work does not start.

## 6.4 Isolation Verification & Pump LOTO

### When Isolation / LOTO is Required
- Any maintenance, nozzle change, hose repair, or entry into a high-pressure system
- When working inside vessels, exchangers, or pipes that could contain residual energy
- When multiple energy sources exist (electrical, mechanical, hydraulic, pneumatic)

### Pump LOTO Minimum Standard (MCR-linked)
1. Shut down pump and relieve all pressure (dump valve open, verified zero pressure)
2. Isolate energy sources (fuel, electrical, starting systems)
3. Apply personal locks and tags (one lock per person)
4. Verify isolation by attempting to start (try-out)
5. Bleed residual pressure and confirm gauges read zero
6. Only then perform the task

**Never rely on a dump valve alone as isolation for maintenance.**

### Isolation Verification for Process Equipment
- Follow client isolation procedures (LOTO, positive isolation, blinds, etc.)
- Double-block-and-bleed or physical disconnection preferred for high-hazard work
- Independent verification by a second competent person strongly recommended

## 6.5 SIMOPS (Simultaneous Operations) Control

**One of the highest RPN items in the FMEA (RPN 200).**

### Mandatory SIMOPS Controls for HPWJ
- Daily SIMOPS coordination meeting when other activities are planned nearby
- Clear zoning and physical barriers between HPWJ exclusion zone and other work
- Specific controls when hot work is nearby (jet spray/mist can carry flammable materials or create ignition risk)
- Vacuum truck, lifting, scaffolding, and electrical work interfaces identified and controlled
- Named SIMOPS coordinator for complex turnarounds
- Stop-work authority if SIMOPS controls break down

**MCR-028 is non-negotiable.**

## 6.6 Pre-Job Briefing (Toolbox Talk)

Must cover (minimum):
- Scope and exact method
- Pressure and tooling
- All high-priority MCR controls that apply today
- Pre-use inspection findings
- Exclusion zones and escape routes
- Communication protocol and emergency signals
- Injection injury response and location of Medical Alert Cards + trauma kit
- SIMOPS interfaces
- Heat stress / weather plan (KSA critical)
- Stop-work authority reminder

All team members must sign the briefing record.

---

## 6.7 Safe Start-Up Sequence (Summary)

1. Pre-use inspection completed and signed
2. Permit active and isolations verified
3. Barricading and exclusion zones in place
4. Team briefed and competent
5. Communication confirmed
6. Dump valve confirmed open / system pressureless
7. Controlled pressure build-up under operator command
8. Continuous monitoring begins

---

## 6.8 During Execution – Continuous Controls

- Operator always maintains control of pressure relief
- Reaction force continuously managed (MCR-016)
- AWD used correctly on all flex lance work (MCR-019/020)
- Whip checks remain in place
- Communication maintained
- Regular visual checks of hoses and fittings
- Immediate stop if any abnormality appears

---

## 6.9 Controlled Shutdown & Handover

1. Reduce pressure to zero under operator control
2. Confirm system pressureless
3. Secure pump (LOTO if leaving unattended)
4. Decontaminate tools and hoses as required
5. Post-job visual inspection
6. Complete all records (checklist, permit close-out, any incidents/near-misses)
7. Handover to next shift or client with clear status

---

## 6.10 Records & Continuous Improvement

- Completed Pre-Use Checklist
- Signed JSA / TRA
- Permit close-out
- Any near-miss or lesson learned → feed into MCR and FMEA within 30 days
- Competency verification records (Chapter 13)

---

## 6.11 Never Rules – Work Process

- Never start without a valid permit and completed pre-use inspection.
- Never start without verified isolation when required.
- Never ignore SIMOPS interfaces.
- Never allow untrained or expired personnel to operate.
- Never leave the pump running unattended.
- Never bypass stop-work authority.
