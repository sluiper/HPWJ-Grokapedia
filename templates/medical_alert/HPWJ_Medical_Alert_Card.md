**HIGH-PRESSURE WATER INJECTION INJURY**

**This is a MEDICAL EMERGENCY**

Small external wound can hide massive internal damage.

**IMMEDIATE ACTIONS**:
1. Stop work & relieve pressure immediately
2. Do NOT probe, squeeze, or remove stuck clothing
3. Apply tourniquet (if trained & bleeding is severe)
4. Evacuate to hospital **NOW** — do not delay
5. Tell medical staff: "High-pressure water injection injury — requires urgent surgical exploration"

**Carry this card at all times when working with HPWJ equipment.**

**BACK — For Medical Personnel (Critical Information)**

**HIGH-PRESSURE WATER INJECTION INJURY**

**Mechanism**: Industrial water jetting at high pressure (often 10,000–40,000+ psi). Fluid and debris driven along tissue planes, tendon sheaths, and neurovascular bundles.

**Clinical Reality**:
- External wound often small/pinhole
- Pain, swelling, and neurological deficit can progress rapidly
- High risk of compartment syndrome, infection, necrosis, and amputation if not treated aggressively

**Recommended Management**:
- **Urgent surgical exploration and decompression** (do not treat as simple laceration)
- Broad-spectrum IV antibiotics + tetanus
- X-ray / imaging to assess extent (but do not delay surgery for imaging if clinical signs are clear)
- Monitor closely for compartment syndrome
- Hand/Plastic/Trauma surgery involvement early
